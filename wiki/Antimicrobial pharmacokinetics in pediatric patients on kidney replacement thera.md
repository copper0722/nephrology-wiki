# Antimicrobial Pharmacokinetics in Pediatric KRT

## Overview
Antimicrobial dosing in pediatric patients on **Kidney Replacement Therapy (KRT)** is complex due to the intersection of **pediatric physiology** (ontogeny, body composition) and **extracorporeal clearance**. Most pediatric dosing is extrapolated from adult data, which is often inaccurate due to differences in **Volume of Distribution (Vd)** and **Clearance (CL)**.

## Exam Logic
*   **The "Pediatric Trap":** A common distractor is the assumption that pediatric dosing is simply a weight-based version of adult dosing. **Incorrect.** Infants/young children have expanded **total body water** and **extracellular water**, significantly increasing the Vd for hydrophilic drugs.
*   **Clearance Determinants:** Drug removal during KRT is driven by:
    1.  **Molecular Weight (MW):** Smaller molecules are removed more easily.
    2.  **Protein Binding:** Only the **free (unbound) fraction** is dialyzable.
    3.  **Effluent Flow Rate (Qef):** In CKRT, higher Qef → ↑ drug clearance.
*   **Beta-Lactam Dynamics:** These are **time-dependent** antibiotics. The goal is $fT > MIC$ (time free concentration remains above minimum inhibitory concentration). In high-intensity CKRT, standard bolus dosing often fails; **extended infusions** or **continuous infusions (CI)** are required to maintain therapeutic levels.
*   **Toxicity Risks:** 
    *   **Cefepime:** High concentrations → **Neurotoxicity**.
    *   **Vancomycin:** High concentrations → **Ototoxicity** and **Nephrotoxicity**.

## Pharmacokinetic Profiles by Agent

### Cefepime
*   **Properties:** Low MW, low protein binding (20%) → **Highly susceptible to CKRT clearance**.
*   **Clinical Pearl:** Clearance correlates strongly with **Qef** (accounts for ≈77% of variance).
*   **Dosing Strategy:** For high-intensity CKRT or high-MIC pathogens, use **4-h extended infusions** or **24-h CI** to meet targets ($100\% fT > 4 \times MIC$).

### Ceftazidime / Ceftolozane
*   **Ceftazidime-Avibactam:** Small molecule, low protein binding. Standard dosing (e.g., 30–7.5 mg/kg q8h) may be **insufficient** in pediatric KRT patients compared to adults.
*   **Ceftolozane-Tazobactam:** Used for MDR *P. aeruginosa*. High-dose CI may be necessary in critically ill children on CKRT.
