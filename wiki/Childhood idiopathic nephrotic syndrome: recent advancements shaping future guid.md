# Childhood Idiopathic Nephrotic Syndrome (INS)

## Overview
**Idiopathic Nephrotic Syndrome (INS)** is the most common pediatric glomerular disease. Characterized by the classic nephrotic tetrad: **edema**, **heavy proteinuria**, **hypoalbuminemia**, and **hyperlipidemia**.

### Diagnostic Criteria (IPNA 2020/2022)
*   **Proteinuria:** UPCR ≥ 2 mg/mg (≥ 200 mg/mmol).
*   **Albumin:** Serum albumin < 30 g/L.
*   **Idiopathic:** Diagnosis of exclusion (rule out post-infectious GN, IgA nephropathy, lupus nephritis, ANCA-vasculitis).

---

## Pathophysiology
### 1. Immune Dysregulation (The "Circulatory Factor")
*   **Anti-nephrin Autoantibodies:** Recent breakthrough identifying antibodies against **nephrin** (slit-diaphragm component).
*   **Prevalence:** Found in ~80% of children with active INS (higher than in adults).
*   **Histology:** More common in **Minimal Change Disease (MCD)** than primary FSGS.
*   **Clinical Significance:** Levels correlate with disease activity; high levels predict immediate post-transplant recurrence.
*   **Therapeutic Target:** Suggests **Rituximab (anti-CD20)** as a personalized therapy for antibody-positive patients.

### 2. Monogenic Podocytopathies
*   Occurs in ~1/3 of **Steroid-Resistant NS (SRNS)**.
*   **Key Genetic Variants:**
    *   **NPHS1** (Nephrin) & **NPHS2** (Podocin).
    *   **WT1** (Wilms Tumor 1).
    *   **ADCK4** (Coenzyme Q10 biosynthesis pathway).
*   **Clinical Impact:** Genetic diagnosis prevents unnecessary immunosuppression and guides donor selection.

## Clinical Classification & Management

### Steroid-Sensitive NS (SSNS)
**Definition:** Complete remission (UPCR < 20 mg/mmol) after 4–6 weeks of corticosteroids.

*   **Frequently Relapsing (FRNS):** 
    *   ≥ 2 relapses in first 6 months **OR** 
    *   ≥ 3 relapses in any 12-month period.
*   **Steroid-Dependent (SDNS):** 
    *   ≥ 2 consecutive relapses while on steroids **OR** 
    *   Within 14 days of discontinuation.
*   **Management Goal:** Sustain remission while minimizing steroid toxicity (Cushingoid features, growth retardation).

### Steroid-Resistant NS (SRNS)
**Definition:** Failure to achieve remission after initial steroid course.
*   **Risk:** High risk of progression to **End-Stage Kidney Disease (ESKD)**.
*   **Approach:** Intensive immunosuppression or evaluation for monogenic causes.

## Exam Logic
*   **The "Anti-Nephrin" Pivot:** If a question asks about the "circulatory factor" in pediatric MCD, the answer is now **anti-nephrin autoantibodies**. This mimics the PLA2R mechanism in adult Membranous Nephropathy.
*   **FRNS vs. SDNS:** Note the updated IPNA threshold for FRNS (≥ 3 relapses/year, previously ≥ 4). This lower threshold is designed to trigger steroid-sparing agents earlier to protect pediatric growth.
*   **Genetic vs. Immune:** SRNS is the "red flag" for monogenic podocytopathy. If a child is SRNS, the exam logic usually points toward genetic testing (NPHS1/2) rather than escalating steroids.
*   **Rituximab Logic:** Rituximab is increasingly viewed not just as a general immunosuppressant, but as a targeted therapy for the B-cell driven anti-nephrin response.
