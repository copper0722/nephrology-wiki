# Cystic Fibrosis-Related Kidney Disease (CFKD)

## Overview
**Cystic Fibrosis-Related Kidney Disease (CFKD)** is an emerging morbidity in the aging Cystic Fibrosis (CF) population. Historically, CF was viewed as a pulmonary/pancreatic disease; however, the introduction of **High-Efficiency Modulator Therapy (HEMT)** (e.g., Trikafta) has increased life expectancy, shifting the clinical focus toward long-term extrapulmonary complications, specifically **Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)**.

## Clinical Manifestations
Kidney involvement in CF is multifactorial, involving intrinsic genetic defects and extrinsic insults.

### 1. Glomerular Diseases
*   **IgA Nephropathy (IgAN):** Currently the most common glomerular disease in PwCF. Likely linked to chronic mucosal inflammation and liver disease.
*   **AA Amyloidosis:** Historically prevalent due to chronic infection/inflammation; frequency has ↓ with better infection control.
*   **Diabetic Glomerulopathy:** Occurs in the context of **CF-Related Diabetes (CFRD)**. PwCF show higher rates of albuminuria compared to Type 1 DM patients, suggesting synergistic CF-related kidney vulnerability.

### 2. Tubular and Interstitial Disease
*   **Acute Kidney Injury (AKI):** Frequently induced by **aminoglycosides** (e.g., tobramycin), dehydration, or contrast.
*   **Nephrocalcinosis & Nephrolithiasis:** 
    *   **Calcium Oxalate stones** are 3x more common than in the general population.
    *   Driven by **hypercalciuria** and **hypocitraturia**.
    *   Linked to CFTR dysfunction affecting **V-ATPase** and **pendrin** (Cl⁻/HCO₃⁻ exchanger).

### 3. Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
*   **Prevalence:** Affects ~19% of PwCF over age 55.
*   **Risk Factors:** Advancing age, CFRD, long-term aminoglycoside use, and lung transplantation.
*   **Prognosis:** CKD in CF is a strong predictor of premature death, primarily via **cardiovascular complications**.

## Pathophysiology
*   **CFTR (ABCC7) Protein:** An ATP-gated anion channel mediating **Cl⁻** and **HCO₃⁻** secretion (5:1 ratio).
*   **Distribution:** Expressed in most nephron segments (apical membrane) **except the glomeruli**.
*   **Mechanism of Injury:**
    *   **Intrinsic:** CFTR dysfunction impairs tubular differentiation and fluid secretion, reducing "kidney reserve."
    *   **Extrinsic:** Chronic inflammation and nephrotoxic drug exposure act on a vulnerable baseline.
    *   **Modulators:** HEMT (Correctors/Potentiators) may mitigate intrinsic CFTR-related dysfunction, though long-term "off-target" effects are unknown.

## Exam Logic
*   **The "Shift" Concept:** Exams may test the transition of CF from a pediatric fatal disease to an adult chronic disease. The key is that **increased survival $\rightarrow$ increased CKD prevalence**.
*   **Common Distractor:** Attributing all CF kidney failure to aminoglycosides. While a major factor, **IgAN** and **intrinsic CFTR dysfunction** are critical components of the modern CFKD phenotype.
*   **Key Association:** CF $\rightarrow$ Hypercalciuria $\rightarrow$ Nephrocalcinosis (due to pendrin/V-ATPase dysfunction).
*   **Diagnostic Pitfall:** CKD in CF is often "silent" and diagnosed late; eGFR may be normal despite preclinical tubular injury.
