# KRT for Inborn Errors of Metabolism (IEM) in Pediatrics

## Overview
Management of acute metabolic crises in neonates/pediatrics—specifically those causing **hyperammonemia** (Urea Cycle Disorders, Organic Acidemias) and **leucine toxicity** (Maple Syrup Urine Disease/MSUD)—requires rapid detoxification. When pharmacological/dietary measures fail, Kidney Replacement Therapy (KRT) is indicated to prevent irreversible neurological damage.

## Modality Comparison

| Feature | CKRT (Continuous) | HD (Intermittent) | PD (Peritoneal) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mortality** | Lowest (~25%) | Higher (~34%) | Higher (~34%) |
| **Clearance Speed** | Fast (Time to 50% RIA: 6.5h) | Fastest (Trend) | Slowest (Time to 50% RIA: 14.4h) |
| **Hemodynamics** | Stable | Risk of instability | Stable |
| **Metabolite Rebound** | Minimal (Continuous) | High (Intermittent) | Moderate |

**Key Metrics:**
*   **Ammonia Threshold:** Pre-treatment ammonia ≥ 1000 µmol/L is associated with significantly higher mortality (31.94% vs 15.04%).
*   **Efficacy:** All modalities effectively reduce ammonia and leucine, but the *rate* and *stability* of removal differ.

## Exam Logic
*   **The "Why" for CKRT:** In neonates, hemodynamic fragility makes intermittent HD risky. Furthermore, intermittent modalities (HD) often lead to **rebound hyperammonemia** (toxins shifting from intracellular to extracellular space between sessions). CKRT provides steady-state clearance, mitigating this rebound.
*   **Common Distractors:** 
    *   *PD as first-line:* While PD is accessible, it is too slow for critical hyperammonemia (14.4h to 50% reduction vs 6.5h for CKRT).
    *   *HD as gold standard for survival:* While HD has the highest peak clearance, the meta-analysis suggests CKRT has a survival advantage due to hemodynamic control and lack of rebound.
*   **Conceptual Pitfall:** Do not confuse "efficacy in reducing levels" (all modalities work) with "clinical outcome/survival" (CKRT trends better).

## Key Trials/Studies
*   **Raina et al. (2024):** Meta-analysis (n=642). Bottom Line: CKRT associated with lower mortality and faster ammonia reduction compared to PD; comparable efficacy to HD but better hemodynamic stability.
*   **Donn et al. (Historical):** Compared exchange transfusion, PD, and HD. Bottom Line: HD superior to PD/Exchange for UCD-related hyperammonemia (predates modern CKRT data).
