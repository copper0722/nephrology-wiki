# Kidney Transplantation in Primary Mitochondrial Diseases

**Primary mitochondrial diseases** are rare, multisystem disorders caused by dysfunction of the mitochondrial respiratory chain. While historically viewed with caution due to multi-organ involvement, recent evidence confirms that **kidney transplantation (KT)** is a viable and successful therapy for end-stage kidney disease (ESKD) in this population.

### Clinical Overview
*   **Renal Phenotypes:** 
    *   **Proximal tubulopathy** (most common).
    *   **Nephrotic-range proteinuria** or **Steroid-Resistant Nephrotic Syndrome (SRNS)**.
    *   Rapid progression to **CKD/ESKD**, particularly in pediatric cases.
*   **Key Genotypes & Presentation:**
    *   **MT-TL1 (m.3243A>G):** Associated with **MELAS** (Mitochondrial Encephalomyopathy, Lactic Acidosis, and Stroke-like episodes) and **MIDD** (Maternally Inherited Diabetes and Deafness). Typically adult onset; higher risk of post-KT neurological/metabolic deterioration.
    *   **RMND1 (Nuclear DNA):** Early childhood onset (often < 2 years), severe course, high early mortality.
    *   **MT-ATP6:** Associated with **Leigh syndrome**.
*   **Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10) Deficiency:** Distinct from the above; often presents as isolated SRNS. **Crucial:** Some subtypes respond to oral CoQ10 replacement, potentially reversing kidney impairment, making KT a secondary option.

### Transplantation Outcomes
*   **Survival:** Both graft and patient survival are high (**> 90%**).
*   **Viability:** KT is considered a viable replacement therapy regardless of whether the mutation is in mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA) or nuclear DNA (nDNA).
*   **Complications:** Systemic mitochondrial dysfunction persists post-KT; neurological or metabolic decline may occur, especially in **m.3243A>G** variants.

## Exam Logic
*   **The Core Concept:** Mitochondrial disease is **NOT** an absolute contraindication to kidney transplantation.
*   **Common Distractors:** 
    *   *CoQ10 Deficiency:* Examiners may use this to test knowledge of **medical management vs. surgical intervention**. Because CoQ10 replacement can be curative/stabilizing, it is a "distractor" from the standard KT pathway.
    *   *Multi-organ Failure:* While systemic involvement exists, the presence of mitochondrial dysfunction in other organs does not preclude a successful renal graft.
*   **Conceptual Pitfalls:** 
    *   Assuming that the "systemic" nature of the disease leads to poor graft survival. In reality, the graft (donor kidney) is healthy; the risk is **patient survival** and **extra-renal deterioration**, not graft rejection or failure.
    *   Confusing the onset patterns: **RMND1** = Pediatric/Severe; **m.3243A>G** = Adult/Insidious.
