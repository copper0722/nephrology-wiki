# Acute Tubular Necrosis (DynaMed)

## Definition
- Most common form of intrinsic AKI (38-55% of all AKI, ~76% of ICU AKI)
- Ischemic or toxic insult to tubular epithelium
- Types: ischemic ATN (hypotension/shock) vs toxic ATN (nephrotoxins)

## Characteristic Lab Findings
- Urine osmolality < 350-400 mOsm/kg (isosthenuric)
- Urine sediment: muddy brown granular casts, renal tubular epithelial cells, granular casts
- FENa > 2%
- Urine Na > 40 mEq/L
- Urine-to-plasma Cr ratio < 20
- BUN:Cr ratio < 10-15

## Prerenal vs Intrinsic Differentiation Table
| Index | Intrinsic (ATN) | Prerenal |
|---|---|---|
| FENa | > 2% | < 1% |
| FEurea | > 35% | < 35% |
| FE uric acid | > 15% | < 7% |
| BUN:Cr | < 10-15 | > 20 |
| Urine Na | > 40 mEq/L | < 20 mEq/L |
| Urine osmolality | ~300 mOsm/kg | > 500 mOsm/kg |

## Diagnostic Performance
- Muddy brown casts + FENa >= 1%: sensitivity 94%, specificity 100% (Kidney360 2022)
- Muddy brown casts alone: sensitivity 78%, specificity 100%
- FENa >= 1% alone: sensitivity 90%, specificity 71%
- Any granular cast or RTE cells: sensitivity 83%, specificity 77% (CJASN 2008)

## Caveats for Urinary Indices
- CKD, diuretic use: may alter FENa, urine Na, osmolality → FEurea more reliable (less affected by diuretics)
- Rhabdomyolysis, myoglobinuria, hemolysis, sepsis, cirrhosis, heart failure, contrast: may affect urine Na and FENa

## Causes
- Ischemic: circulatory shock, sepsis (most common), transient hypotension, post-surgical
- Toxic medications: NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, cephalosporins, vancomycin, amphotericin B, acyclovir, cisplatin, CNIs, tenofovir, bisphosphonates
- Contrast media
- Pigment: rhabdomyolysis (myoglobin), hemolysis (hemoglobin)
- Crystal: calcium oxalate, uric acid, acyclovir, sulfonamides, indinavir
- Metabolic: hypercalcemia
- Other: tumor lysis syndrome, severe proteinuria (MCD)

## Pathogenesis
- Ischemic/toxic insult → endothelial dysfunction, intrarenal vasoconstriction, coagulation/inflammatory pathway activation, oxidative stress
- Histology: mild interstitial infiltrate, apical blebbing, sloughed epithelium/casts, interstitial edema; true cellular necrosis is rare
- Tamm-Horsfall protein granulomatous reaction possible

## Management
- No specific medications for ATN treatment
- Hemodynamic support: avoid hypotension; fluids vs vasopressors as needed
- Nutrition: enteral preferred; protein 1.5 g/kg/day
- Avoid further nephrotoxic insults
- Dopamine/fenoldopam: may convert oliguric to nonoliguric but NO effect on ATN duration, dialysis need, or survival
- Dialysis: for volume overload, electrolyte disorders, uremia; biocompatible membranes and continuous methods may improve outcomes

## Prevention
- Shock-induced: isotonic crystalloids, vasopressors, tight glucose control [Conditional]
- Medication-induced: avoid nephrotoxins, adjust doses
- Postoperative: hemodynamic/oxygenation optimization, avoid certain medications [Conditional]
