# AKI in Adults - Approach (DynaMed)

## Definition
- KDIGO: SCr increase >= 0.3 mg/dL (>= 26.5 mcmol/L) within 48h, OR SCr increase to >= 1.5x baseline within 7d, OR UO < 0.5 mL/kg/h for 6h
- AKIN: same criteria within 48h window

## Classification

### RIFLE (Crit Care 2004)
- Risk: SCr 1.5x or GFR decrease > 25%; UO < 0.5 mL/kg/h x 6h
- Injury: SCr 2x or GFR decrease > 50%; UO < 0.5 mL/kg/h x 12h
- Failure: SCr 3x or GFR decrease > 75% or SCr > 4 mg/dL (with acute increase >= 0.5); UO < 0.3 mL/kg/h x 24h or anuria x 12h
- Loss: persistent AKI > 4 weeks
- ESKD: > 3 months

### KDIGO Staging
- Stage 1: SCr 1.5-1.9x baseline OR increase >= 0.3 mg/dL; UO < 0.5 mL/kg/h x 6-12h
- Stage 2: SCr 2-2.9x baseline; UO < 0.5 mL/kg/h x >= 12h
- Stage 3: SCr >= 3x baseline OR >= 4 mg/dL (with AKI criteria met) OR initiation of RRT; UO < 0.3 mL/kg/h x >= 24h OR anuria >= 12h
- If UO and SCr lead to different stages, assign higher stage

## Epidemiology
- Incidence > 5,000/million/year (non-dialysis AKI), 295/million/year (dialysis-dependent AKI)
- Pooled prevalence 21.6% in adults (SR, 312 studies, 49M persons)
- ICU: 23-36% prevalence, 12-20% needing RRT
- Community-acquired 58%, hospital-acquired 40% overall

## Etiology Classification
- Prerenal: volume depletion, heart failure, sepsis, hepatorenal syndrome, renal artery stenosis
- Intrinsic: ATN (most common), AIN/ATIN, glomerulonephritis, vasculitis, TMA
- Postrenal: obstruction (stones, BPH, malignancy, retroperitoneal fibrosis)

## Risk Factors
- CKD, hypertension, heart failure, diabetes, chronic liver disease, cancer, anemia, hyperuricemia, albuminuria
- Medications: NSAIDs, contrast, aminoglycosides, amphotericin, vancomycin, cisplatin, CNIs, ACEi/ARBs, PPIs
- Triple whammy: ACEi/ARB + diuretic + NSAID (RR 1.31 for triple therapy)
- Vancomycin + piperacillin-tazobactam: OR 3.4 for AKI vs vancomycin monotherapy

## Evaluation
- Labs: BUN, Cr, electrolytes (Na, K, Ca, Mg, PO4), CBC, LFTs
- Urine: volume, osmolality, Na, Cr (for FENa), dipstick, microscopy
- Imaging: renal US to rule out obstruction [Strong Recommendation]; noncontrast CT if stone suspected
- Biopsy: when GN, vasculitis, or AIN suspected and prerenal/postrenal excluded [Conditional]

## Management
- Hospitalize if advanced evaluation needed, complications present, or not responsive to initial management
- Fluid/electrolyte management; stop nephrotoxins; treat underlying cause
- Nutrition: 20-30 kcal/kg/day [KDIGO 2C]; avoid protein restriction [KDIGO 2D]; enteral preferred [KDIGO 2C]
- Diuretics: NOT for routine AKI treatment [KDIGO 1B]; only for volume overload
- Do NOT use: low-dose dopamine [KDIGO 1A], fenoldopam [KDIGO 2C], ANP [KDIGO 2C]
- RRT: emergently for life-threatening fluid/electrolyte/acid-base changes [Strong]
- Volume repletion: balanced crystalloids preferred over NS (SMART/SALT-ED trials, NEJM 2018)
