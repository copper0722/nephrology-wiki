# AKI Management, Complications, Prevention (DynaMed)

## Management by Cause Table
| Cause | Management |
|---|---|
| Infection/sepsis | IV fluids + antibiotics |
| Acute heart failure | Afterload reduction +/- diuretic |
| Cardiorenal | ACEi/ARB, hydralazine, nitrates; diuresis if volume overload |
| Renovascular | Antihypertensive; angioplasty for bilateral RAS |
| Hepatorenal | Albumin + vasoconstrictors (terlipressin first-line); liver transplant |
| Rhabdomyolysis | Aggressive IV fluids; urine alkalinization pH > 6.5; maintain UO > 300 mL/h |
| AIN | Stop offending drug; steroids if severe/unresponsive |
| Acute GN | Immunosuppression (steroids +/- cyclophosphamide/MMF) |
| Atheroembolism | D/C anticoagulation if spontaneous; avoid intraarterial procedures |
| Increased IAP | Decompression (NG tube, drainage, surgery if IAP >= 20 mmHg) |
| ACEi/ARB | Temporary discontinuation |
| NSAID | D/C NSAIDs; isotonic crystalloid |
| TMA | Plasma exchange |
| Cast nephropathy | Chemo (steroids + cyclophosphamide/bortezomib) |
| Pyelonephritis | IV antibiotics |

## Fluid Management
- Volume depletion: IV crystalloids (balanced preferred > NS) [KDIGO 2B for isotonic crystalloids]
- Colloids may be used in liver failure or burns
- Fluid overload: loop diuretics (IV, bolus or infusion); avoid fluid overload → pulmonary edema, IAH

## BICARICU-2 Trial (JAMA 2025)
- 640 adults with severe metabolic acidemia (pH <= 7.2) + moderate-severe AKI
- NaHCO3 to target pH >= 7.3 vs no NaHCO3
- 90-day mortality: 62.1% vs 61.7% (NS)
- RRT by day 28: 35% vs 50% (RD -15.5%, 95% CI -23.1 to -7.8%) → NaHCO3 may reduce RRT need
- Consistent with BICAR-ICU (benefit only in AKI subgroup)

## Diuretics
- NOT for routine AKI [KDIGO 2C]; NOT to reduce dialysis need
- Loop diuretics: no effect on mortality, renal recovery, or dialysis rate (3 SRs)
- May convert oliguric to nonoliguric; may shorten oliguric period (WMD -7.7 days)
- Contraindicated in anuria

## Medications NOT Recommended
- Low-dose dopamine: no benefit (SR, 61 RCTs, 3,359 patients) [KDIGO 1A]
- Fenoldopam: hypotension risk; may improve markers but no clinical outcomes [KDIGO 2C]
- ANP: low-dose may reduce RRT need (NNT 3-102) but high-dose causes hypotension [KDIGO 2B]
- rhIGF-1: not recommended [KDIGO 1B]
- Thyroid hormones: may increase mortality

## Nutrition
- Energy: 20-30 kcal/kg/day [KDIGO 2C]
- Protein: 0.8-1 g/kg/day (noncatabolic, no dialysis); 1-1.5 g/kg/day (on RRT); up to 1.7 g/kg/day (CRRT/hypercatabolic)
- Enteral route preferred [KDIGO 2C]
- Carbohydrate 3-5 g/kg, fat 0.8-1 g/kg

## Anticoagulants in AKI
- Caution: uremic platelet dysfunction + reduced excretion of LMWH/DOACs
- Uremia + active bleeding: desmopressin 0.3 mcg/kg, cryoprecipitate, conjugated estrogens, or dialysis without anticoagulation

## RRT Indications
- Life-threatening fluid/electrolyte/acid-base changes [KDIGO Not Graded]
- Consider entire clinical scenario, not BUN/Cr thresholds alone [KDIGO Not Graded]
- Modalities: IRRT (IHD 4h, 3-4x/week), SLED (6-12h, 6-7d/week), CRRT (24h continuous: CVVH, CVVHD, CVVHDF)
- Anticoagulation: regional citrate preferred for CRRT [KDIGO 2B]; heparin for IRRT [KDIGO 1C]
- Buffer: bicarbonate preferred over lactate [KDIGO 2C]; especially in circulatory shock [KDIGO 1B]
- Vascular access: R IJ > femoral > L IJ [KDIGO Not Graded]; US-guided [KDIGO 1A]

## Abdominal Compartment Syndrome
- IAP > 12 mmHg = intra-abdominal hypertension
- IAP > 20 mmHg + new organ dysfunction = abdominal compartment syndrome
- Measure via transbladder technique
- Treatment: sedation, NG decompression, drainage, avoid fluid overload; surgery if IAP >= 20 mmHg

## Prevention
- Shock: isotonic crystalloids, vasopressors, tight glucose control [Conditional]
- Medication-induced: avoid nephrotoxins, dose adjustment
- Postoperative: hemodynamic/oxygenation optimization
- Contrast: periprocedural hydration [Strong], avoid nephrotoxins, identify high-risk patients
