# Cardiology Arrhythmia — Journal Reading Wiki

## Pacemakers (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/EVIDra2400323
Design: Narrative review
Key: Modern pacemakers (leadless, His-bundle, LBBAP) transformed bradycardia and heart block management; >1M devices implanted annually with expanding HF applications.
Clinical: Definitive therapy for irreversible bradycardia/conduction disease; cornerstone of modern cardiac care; emerging role in HF with reduced EF via CRT.
Numbers: >1M annual implantations globally; growth trajectory with population aging.

## Increasing Potassium in High-Risk VT Patients (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2509542
Design: RCT (POTCAST trial, investigator-initiated, event-driven)
Key: Targeted elevation of plasma potassium to high-normal range (4.5–5.0 mmol/L) vs standard care in ICD patients; low-normal K+ (3.5–4.0) associated with increased arrhythmia mortality.
Clinical: Modifiable electrolyte strategy via dietary guidance, K+ supplementation, and MRA for VT risk reduction in post-ICD populations.

## Caffeinated Coffee and Atrial Fibrillation (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.21056
Design: RCT (DECAF trial, open-label, prospective)
Key: Caffeinated coffee ≥1 cup/day reduced AF/flutter recurrence 39% vs abstinence post-cardioversion; challenges traditional proarrhythmic belief.
Clinical: AF/flutter patients post-cardioversion can continue coffee safely; may confer protective effect; contradicts long-standing clinical caution.
Numbers: n=200, age 69±11 yr (71% male); recurrence 47% (coffee) vs 64% (abstinence); HR 0.61 (95% CI 0.42–0.89), P=.01.

## Cardiac Arrest in Long-Distance Races (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.3026
Design: Prospective observational case series (RACER registry 2010–2023)
Key: Cardiac arrest incidence stable (0.60/100K participants) vs 2000–2009, but case fatality halved (71%→34%); improved emergency response and AED accessibility drove survival gains.
Clinical: Marathon/half-marathon participants face cardiac arrest risk; male and full-marathon runners highest risk; CAD now leading etiology (not HCM); rapid CPR + shockable rhythm predict survival.
Numbers: 29.3M race participations analyzed (3× vs 2000–2009); 443 races; cardiac death 0.20/100K; case fatality 34%; male and marathon cohorts at higher risk.
