# Cardiology Atherosclerosis — Journal Reading Wiki

## SHARP Trial (2011)
DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(11)60739-3
Design: RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Key: Simvastatin + ezetimibe reduces major atherosclerotic events 17% in CKD (RR 0.83, p=0.0021); benefit driven by stroke (RR 0.75) & revascularization (RR 0.79), not MI
Clinical: LDL reduction effective in CKD despite advanced renal disease; myopathy risk minimal (2/10K patient-years)
Numbers: N=9270 CKD, FU 4.9yr median, events 11.3% vs 13.4%, LDL ↓0.85 mmol/L

## Lp(a) in South Asian ASCVD (2025)
DOI: 10.1161/JAHA.124.040361
Design: Narrative review
Key: South Asians have higher Lp(a) elevation prevalence (25% >50 mg/dL); MI occurs ~10yr earlier than Europeans; disproportionate CAD burden
Clinical: Ethnicity-specific risk factor; Lp(a) screening recommended in SA populations; novel Lp(a) drugs may reduce premature ASCVD
Numbers: 25% SA with Lp(a) >50 mg/dL; MI onset 10yr earlier in SA; 1.9B people affected

## Aspirin for High Lp(a) (2025)
DOI: 10.1007/s11883-025-01296-w
Design: Contemporary review
Key: Primary prevention aspirin in Lp(a) ≥50 mg/dL reduces CHD/ASCVD death ~50% (observational); post-hoc RCT analyses support benefit in high-risk LPA genotypes
Clinical: Emerging evidence supports aspirin use; requires shared decision-making (bleeding vs benefit); RCT validation pending
Numbers: CHD/ASCVD mortality ↓50% with aspirin (Lp(a) ≥50); meta-analysis aspirin NNT=241, NNH=210

## Lepodisiran (siRNA-Lp(a)) (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2415818
Design: Phase II RCT (ALPACA)
Key: Single lepodisiran injection achieves Lp(a) ↓93.9% (placebo-adjusted) sustained 540d; well-tolerated; Phase III outcomes trial ongoing
Clinical: First effective Lp(a)-targeted drug; hepatic apolipoprotein(a) mRNA suppression; transforms Lp(a) management from intractable to modifiable
Numbers: Lp(a) ↓93.9% at 60-180d post-injection; adverse events mild, injection-site only

## Alcohol & Lipid Profiles (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2025.0583
Design: Cohort study (longitudinal annual health checkup database)
Key: Examines lipid response to natural alcohol behavior changes (initiation vs cessation) in real-world setting; Japanese population
Clinical: Real-world alcohol-lipid associations outside controlled interventions; population-based evidence for counseling
Numbers: N=57,691 individuals, 328,676 checkup records, FU 10yr, mean age 46.8yr, 53% female

## Lp(a): Biomarker to Therapy (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.amjcard.2025.02.034
Design: Narrative review
Key: Lp(a) independent ASCVD/calcific AS risk; ASO/siRNA agents reduce Lp(a) >90% Phase I; continuous CVD risk without threshold
Clinical: Comprehensive pathophysiology (atherogenesis, inflammation, calcification, thrombosis); evolving screening guidelines; lifestyle/statins ineffective for Lp(a)
Numbers: Lp(a) >75th percentile ↑AS/MI; >90th percentile ↑HF; Lp(a) >180 mg/dL risk ≈ heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia

## Lipoprotein(a) (JAMA Clinical Review) (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.2373
Design: Clinical review
Key: Lp(a) ≥50 mg/dL → ASCVD 80.0 events/1000 person-yr vs <15 mg/dL → 55.3 events/1000 py (aHR 1.35); linear dose-response, no threshold
Clinical: Gene-determined trait; independent of ethnicity & conventional risk factors; novel Lp(a) drugs in trials (ASO, siRNA)
Numbers: Per 20 mg/dL Lp(a) increment HR 1.11; Lp(a) 50 vs 7 mg/dL → 40% relative risk ↑
