# General Medicine — Journal Reading Wiki

## Targeting B Cells and Plasma Cells in Glomerular Disease (2025)
DOI: 10.1681/ASN.0000000772
Design: Narrative review
Key: B-cell and plasma-cell depletion strategies (anti-CD20, BAFF/APRIL inhibition, proteasome inhibitors, CAR-T) offer targeted alternatives to broad immunosuppression in antibody-mediated glomerular disease.
Clinical: Guides personalized therapy selection for IgAN, membranous disease, C3GN; addresses steroid/immunosuppressive resistance.
Numbers: N/A

## IgA Nephropathy and Glomerular Diseases Associated with Infection (2025)
DOI: 10.58483/nsap.2025.24.1
Design: Review/self-assessment program
Key: Mucosal infections trigger IgAN flares; IgA-dominant infection-related GN (IgAD-IRGN) is distinct entity with different natural history; emerging therapies target mucosal IgA production.
Clinical: Distinguishing IgAN from IgAD-IRGN critical—management and prognosis diverge; most common in immunosuppressed elderly.
Numbers: N/A

## Desensitization in HLA-incompatible Kidney Transplant Recipients (2025)
DOI: 10.1093/ckj/sfaf219
Design: Narrative review
Key: Novel desensitization agents (imlifidase, IL-6 inhibitors, plasma cell-directed therapies, complement inhibitors, CAR-T) improve upon traditional IVIG/plasmapheresis for sensitized transplant candidates.
Clinical: Expands transplant access; provides decision framework for when desensitization likely to succeed vs futile.
Numbers: N/A

## Maternal Stress Triggers Early-life Eczema via Fetal Mast Cell Programming (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-09419-8
Design: Animal study (mouse prenatal stress model)
Key: Prenatal stress elevates amniotic corticosterone, reprograms fetal skin mast cells and mechanosensory neurons, causing dysfunctional neuroimmune circuits that trigger postnatal eczema on mechanical friction.
Clinical: Explains prenatal origins of atopic dermatitis; therapeutic targets: maternal cortisol normalization or mast cell Mcpt5 knockout.
Numbers: Stress E13–E18; elevated IL-7, CXCL9, IL-5, IL-9 in offspring serum; increased TEWL at W3 and W8.

## Which Patients with Heart Failure Benefit from SGLT2 Inhibitors? (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/EVIDccon2400446
Design: Evidence-based narrative review
Key: SGLT2i reduces composite CV death/HF hospitalization regardless of EF or diabetes; class 1A indication for all adult HF patients despite slow real-world adoption.
Clinical: Only 15–20% eligible patients receive SGLT2i; knowledge translation urgently needed; counsel all HF candidates.
Numbers: 15–20% adoption rate; EMPA-REG, CANVAS, EMPEROR-R/P, DAPA-HF, DELIVER, EMPULSE trials support.

## KDIGO 2025 Clinical Practice Guideline for IgAN and IgAV Management (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2025.04.004
Design: Clinical practice guideline (update of 2021 version)
Key: IgAN diagnosed by kidney biopsy only (no validated serum/urine biomarker); International IgAN Prediction Tool quantifies 7-year progression risk; MEST-C classification guides prognosis.
Clinical: Risk stratification informs treatment intensity; new therapies target mucosal IgA production and complement activation.
Numbers: N/A

## KDIGO 2025 Clinical Practice Guideline for Nephrotic Syndrome in Children (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2024.11.007
Design: Clinical practice guideline (update of 2021 version)
Key: Updated evidence-based management of steroid-sensitive and steroid-resistant childhood nephrotic syndrome; complete remission = uPCR ≤20 mg/mmol on 3 consecutive days.
Clinical: Distinguishes relapse patterns (infrequent vs frequent) to guide maintenance therapy escalation.
Numbers: Nephrotic-range proteinuria ≥200 mg/mmol uPCR or ≥1000 mg/m²/d; remission ≤20 mg/mmol; albumin <30 g/L defines NS.

## Foley Catheter Management: A Review (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jamasurg.2025.0565
Design: Clinical review
Key: Comprehensive review of catheter indications, selection, insertion/removal, and maintenance strategies to minimize CAUTI, encrustation, spasm, and catheter-related pain.
Clinical: Establishes appropriate vs inappropriate indications; removes catheters promptly postoperatively unless specific indication persists.
Numbers: ~100M catheters/year globally; ~20% US hospitalized patients catheterized.

## Nephrectomy in Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease: Consensus Statement (2025)
DOI: 10.1093/ndt/gfaf019
Design: Consensus statement (systematic review + meta-analysis + Delphi survey of 3 rounds)
Key: 23 evidence-based practice points establish indications for ADPKD nephrectomy: lack of transplant space (42.6%), pain (31.3%), recurrent UTI (22.9%), hemorrhage (14.4%).
Clinical: Clarifies which ADPKD patients benefit and optimal timing/surgical technique; reduces practice variation.
Numbers: 54 studies, 1955 procedures analyzed; Clavien-Dindo ≥3 = major complication.

## Brand-new Colour Created by Tricking Human Eyes with Laser (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/d41586-025-01252-3
Design: Research brief/proof-of-concept
Key: Novel "Oz" system selectively stimulates retinal S-, M-, and L-cone cells with laser to create previously imperceptible color ("olo") with supersaturated blue-green hue.
Clinical: Potential future application in color blindness; proof-of-concept only in 5 participants; high technical barriers, limited FOV.
Numbers: 5 participants exposed; FOV ~2× lunar diameter; three cone types manipulated.

## Safety and Efficacy of Senolytic UBX1325 in Diabetic Macular Edema (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/EVIDoa2400009
Design: RCT (phase 2, randomized, double-blind, sham-controlled, 48-week)
Key: Single intravitreal UBX1325 (BCL-XL senolytic) in anti-VEGF–refractory DME shows safety; efficacy trend of +5.6 ETDRS letters vs sham (95% CI −1.5 to 12.7, not significant).
Clinical: Targets senescent cells as alternative mechanism in resistant DME; larger trials needed to confirm benefit.
Numbers: 10 µg dose; 48-week follow-up; BCVA trend +5.6 letters; no dose-limiting toxicity.

## Cutaneous Leishmaniasis (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMicm2500605
Design: Case report
Key: 3-year-old with *Leishmania guyanensis* cutaneous leishmaniasis presenting as painless ulcer with rolled borders and satellite papules; diagnosed by biopsy histology + PCR.
Clinical: Climate change and migration expanding geographic range; can cause disfiguring scars; miltefosine effective treatment.
Numbers: 6-week presentation; *L. guyanensis* confirmed by PCR; recent migrant from Venezuela.
