## Platelet Dysfunction in Renal Failure (2004)
DOI: 10.1055/s-2004-835678
Design: Narrative review
Key: ESRD patients exhibit bleeding diathesis from platelet dysfunction driven by uremic toxins; dialysis improves but cannot fully correct abnormalities. Paradoxically, thrombotic complications remain frequent despite impaired hemostasis.
Clinical: Management of bleeding risk in dialysis; incomplete correction requires adjunctive interventions (hematocrit, desmopressin).
Numbers: ~1/3 of uremic patients have GI bleeding; modern dialysis and EPO reduced but did not eliminate severe bleeding events.

## Daratumumab in Relapsed or Refractory Pediatric Immune Thrombocytopenia (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMc2501527
Design: Phase 2 open-label single-arm trial
Key: Anti-CD38 monoclonal antibody daratumumab achieved 90% platelet response in children with relapsed/refractory ITP by 8 weeks; 86% response in rituximab-refractory cases.
Clinical: Novel therapy for ITP children resistant to conventional treatments; rapid response onset (median 1 week) expands second-line options.
Numbers: N=20 children; 90% response (18/20) at 8 weeks; median response time 1 week; 50% sustained response at 24 weeks.

Key: Two hypothalamic MPN neuron populations regulate social homeostasis: isolation-responsive neurons drive social seeking, reunion-responsive neurons signal satiety. Touch is critical sensory modality for assessing social presence and satisfying social need.
Clinical: Reveals neural circuits maintaining social homeostasis; implications for understanding social isolation pathology and psychiatric disease mechanisms.
Numbers: N/A (basic mouse neuroscience research).

## Performance of Dialysis Facilities After Health-Equity Scoring Incentive (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMc2413208
Design: Observational policy analysis
Key: Health-equity scoring adjustment in CMS ETC model reduced financial penalties for high-social-risk facilities but failed to narrow gaps in home dialysis utilization or transplant rates between high/low-risk facilities.
Clinical: Demonstrates limitations of single-indicator equity adjustments; comprehensive design needed to reduce ESRD treatment disparities.
Numbers: N=2189 dialysis facilities studied (2021–2022); reduced penalties for high-risk facilities but no improvement in utilization/transplant gap closure.

## Stress Dynamically Modulates Neuronal Autophagy to Gate Depression Onset (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-08807-4
Design: Basic neuroscience mechanistic study
Key: Chronic stress impairs lateral habenula autophagy, promoting depression; enhancing LHb autophagy reverses this via degradation of glutamate receptors, restoring neuronal homeostasis and producing rapid antidepressant effects.
Clinical: Identifies LHb autophagy as common antidepressant target; autophagy enhancers may provide rapid-acting depression therapy.
Numbers: Paroxetine/ketamine restore LHb autophagy; effect LHb-specific (8 other stress-related brain regions unaffected); mechanism: demand-degradation of glutamate receptors.

## Dopamine Induces Fear Extinction by Activating Reward-Responding Amygdala Neurons (2025)
DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2501331122
Design: Neuroscience mechanistic study
Key: VTA dopamine neurons bidirectionally control fear extinction in amygdala: activating DA→BLA-reward neurons accelerates extinction, while activating DA→BLA-fear neurons impairs it via differential dopamine receptor expression.
Clinical: Elucidates neural basis of fear extinction relevant to PTSD and anxiety disorders; dopamine signaling as therapeutic target.
Numbers: N/A (animal mechanistic studies with fiber photometry and optogenetic manipulation).

Numbers: Tested on 3–5 different proteins; 10-minute editing window; currently requires customization; limited to cellular applications.

## Neonatal Fc Receptor – Biology and Therapeutics (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra2312718
Design: Comprehensive review
Key: FcRn protects IgG and albumin from degradation, maintaining 21-day half-life; FcRn inhibitors (efgartigimod, rozanolixizumab) accelerate IgG clearance for IgG-mediated autoimmune diseases including MG, CIDP, ITP.
Clinical: Novel mechanism for treating autoimmune diseases via targeted IgG reduction; complementary to B-cell targeting strategies.
Numbers: IgG/albumin half-life ~21 days; FcRn inhibitors approved/in trials for multiple autoimmune conditions; efficacy varies by disease.

## Sleep Pressure Accumulates in a Voltage-Gated Lipid Peroxidation Memory (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-08734-4
Design: Basic neuroscience mechanistic study
Key: Sleep pressure accumulates via lipid peroxidation of neuronal membranes; Kv channel β-subunit (Hk) acts as dynamic redox memory where NADP+ accumulation during wakefulness drives sleep drive via ROS-induced PUFA oxidation.
Clinical: Reveals molecular basis for sleep homeostasis; sleep deprivation causes membrane PUFA depletion; implications for sleep disorders and neuroprotection.
Numbers: 51 lipid species change >2-fold with sleep deprivation; 18 confirmed by HPLC-MS²; PUFA reduction in sleep-deprived brain vs. PUFA preservation in rested brain.

## The Fetal-to-Adult Hemoglobin Switch: Mechanism and Therapy (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra2405260
Design: Comprehensive review
Key: BCL11A is master regulator of fetal→adult hemoglobin switch via γ-globin silencing; BCL11A erythroid enhancer editing permits HbF reactivation for treatment of β-thalassemia and sickle cell disease.
Clinical: Gene editing therapy now approved for β-thalassemia and SCD; opens molecular medicine era for heritable blood disorders; challenges remain for universal applicability.
Numbers: >500K infants/year born with SCD; >50% die by age 5 in sub-Saharan Africa; gene-editing therapies approved for clinical use.

## Anticoagulation or Antiplatelet Therapy for Device-Detected Atrial Fibrillation (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMclde2411897
Design: Clinical decision / expert debate
Key: Device-detected subclinical AF increases stroke risk 2.5-fold; oral anticoagulation reduces ischemic stroke 32% vs. antiplatelet therapy; absolute risk context-dependent (1.7%/year for subclinical AF).
Clinical: Guides management of asymptomatic/subclinical AF detected by wearables/implanted devices; anticoagulation generally recommended for CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥2.
Numbers: CHA₂DS₂-VASc score 5 (high-risk); device-detected AF increases stroke risk 2.5×; absolute risk 1.7%/year; anticoagulation provides 32% relative risk reduction.

## Structural Dynamics of DNA Unwinding by a Replicative Helicase (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-08766-w
Design: Structural biology mechanistic study
Key: Cryo-EM structures of SV40 LTag helicase reveal ATP hydrolysis as "entropy switch" enabling strand separation; tracking strand pulled through central channel; non-tracking strand exits posteriorly via self-separating wedge mechanism.
Clinical: Mechanistic insights into viral DNA replication; potential antiviral targeting strategies; implications for understanding cancer cell replication fork dynamics.
Numbers: 3.1 Å cryo-EM resolution; DNA binding induces asymmetric C-layer rotation; one nucleotide per subunit increment; separated strand exit pathways defined.
