## Dying Cells Fan the Flames of Inflammation (2021)
DOI: 10.1126/science.abi5934
Design: Narrative review
Key: Programmed cell death (apoptosis, necroptosis, pyroptosis) releases pro-inflammatory DAMPs, driving tissue damage. Inhibiting lytic cell death via RIPK1/NLRP3/GSDMD blockade as therapeutic strategy.
Clinical: Rheumatoid arthritis, atherosclerosis, IBD, COVID-19 — potential for anti-inflammatory therapeutics.
Numbers: N/A

## Current Status of HCV Elimination in Taiwan (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.jfma.2025.09.031
Design: Narrative review
Key: Taiwan anti-HCV prevalence 2.86%; govt policy 2017–2024 overcame diagnostic/care barriers. Achieved 90.6% diagnostic rate & 92.8% treatment rate in general population — met WHO programmatic targets.
Clinical: National elimination strategy success; pathway for other HCV-endemic regions.
Numbers: 188,126 patients needing treatment; diagnosis rate 90.6%; treatment rate 92.8%

## Efficacy of Baloxavir Treatment in Preventing Influenza Transmission (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2413156
Design: RCT (Phase 3b, double-blind, placebo-controlled)
Key: Single-dose baloxavir significantly reduced PCR-confirmed influenza transmission vs placebo in household contacts; no significant reduction in symptomatic transmission. Drug-resistant variants detected.
Clinical: Antiviral treatment to reduce transmission; household contact prophylaxis.
Numbers: N/A

## Phase 3 Trial of DPP-1 Inhibitor Brensocatib in Bronchiectasis (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2411664
Design: RCT (Phase 3 ASPEN trial)
Key: Brensocatib 25 mg daily significantly reduced annualized exacerbation rate vs placebo; slowed FEV₁ decline. Mechanism: DPP-1 inhibition reduces neutrophil-mediated inflammation.
Clinical: Non-CF bronchiectasis; new anti-inflammatory therapy targeting neutrophil elastase pathway.
Numbers: Exacerbation rate reduction (specific % from primary analysis needed)

## An HIV Vaccine in the Era of Lenacapavir for PrEP (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMp2415893
Design: Perspective/Editorial
Key: Lenacapavir (6-monthly injection) provides 96–100% HIV protection; however, sole reliance limited by short duration, access barriers, resistance risk. Combined long-acting PrEP + vaccine optimal.
Clinical: HIV prevention strategy integration; vaccine development rationale persists.
Numbers: PURPOSE trials: 96–100% efficacy; PURPOSE 2 vs F/TDF: 89% relative risk reduction

## BCG Revaccination for Prevention of Mycobacterium tuberculosis Infection (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2412381
Design: RCT (Phase 2b, double-blind, placebo-controlled, South Africa)
Key: BCG revaccination in QFT-negative adolescents failed to prevent persistent TB infection. Vaccine efficacy point estimate –3.8% over median 30 months follow-up.
Clinical: TB vaccine development challenge; revaccination strategy ineffective in this population.
Numbers: Vaccine efficacy –3.8%; median follow-up 30 months

## To BCG or Not Two BCG (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMe2502491
Design: Editorial/Commentary
Key: Prior Phase 2 trial showed BCG revaccination 45% efficacy vs persistent IGRA conversion; current trial failed to replicate. Highlights need to understand T-cell protective immunity mechanisms for TB vaccine design.
Clinical: TB vaccine development; endpoint selection (infection vs progression to disease).
Numbers: Prior trial VE 45% for persistent IGRA; current VE –3.8%

## Macrophages Direct Location-Dependent Recall of B Cell Memory (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2025.04.005
Design: Mixed methods (murine models + human booster trial)
Key: Draining lymph node (dLN) resident memory B cells preferentially re-enter germinal centers upon re-challenge. dLN subcapsular sinus macrophages coordinate this location-dependent recall. Ipsilateral BNT162b2 booster faster bNAb generation.
Clinical: Vaccine booster site selection; memory B-cell optimization for variant neutralization.
Numbers: N/A

## Bifidobacteria Support Optimal Infant Vaccine Responses (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-08796-4
Design: Prospective cohort (n=191) + preclinical mouse models
Key: Early-life antibiotic exposure (neonatal, not perinatal) associated with reduced vaccine antibody titers at 7 months (PCV13, 6-in-1 components). Low Bifidobacterium abundance linked to poor response. Microbiota restoration recovers vaccine immunity.
Clinical: Antibiotic stewardship in neonates; microbiota-based interventions to preserve vaccine efficacy.
Numbers: Reduced antibody titers in Neo-ABX vs control groups (fold-change data needed)

Clinical: Germinal center dynamics; B-cell selection & antibody quality.
