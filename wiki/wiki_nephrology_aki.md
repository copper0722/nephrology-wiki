## How to Communicate Medical Numbers (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.13655
Design: JAMA Insights (opinion/editorial)
Key: Effective medical communication depends on patient numeracy; only 34% of US adults can perform simple numerical tasks. Provides 5 evidence-based strategies (absolute risk, consistent denominators, part-to-whole visuals, etc.) to improve patient understanding.
Clinical: Essential guide for shared decision-making; low numeracy overlaps with low literacy (97%) and incomplete secondary education (81%).
Numbers: 34/4,637 US adults can complete basic numerical tasks; 97% of lowest-numeracy adults have lowest literacy

## ASN Kidney Health Guidance: Outpatient Management of Dialysis-Requiring Acute Kidney Injury (2025)
DOI: 10.1681/ASN.0000000646
Design: Clinical guideline (multidisciplinary expert consensus)
Key: 30% of AKI-D survivors discharged on dialysis; face significantly higher risk of permanent dialysis dependence, CKD, cardiovascular disease, readmission, and mortality. Addresses major care gaps in US outpatient practice where AKI patients often receive ESRD-based protocols despite different pathophysiology.
Clinical: Standardizes outpatient AKI-D management; corrects divergence between guideline recommendations and actual practice variability.
Numbers: AKI patients in ESRD facilities: 6,400 (2017) → 11,964 (2020); 30% remain dialysis-dependent post-discharge
