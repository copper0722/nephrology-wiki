# Chronic Kidney Disease -- Staging, Complications, and Cardiovascular Risk

## Exam Logic Summary

CKD is a foundational topic. The exam tests: (1) KDIGO staging system (GFR + albuminuria = 2-axis grid), (2) when to refer/initiate RRT, (3) pharmacotherapy hierarchy (ACEi/ARB, SGLT2i, GLP-1RA, finerenone), (4) CVD risk stratification unique to CKD, (5) complication management (anemia, MBD, acidosis, hyperkalemia). Common distractor: confusing GFR categories (G3a vs G3b cutoff at 45), or failing to account for albuminuria category in risk stratification.

**Textbook ref:** Brenner Ch. 52 (CKD), Daugirdas Ch. 1-2, KDIGO 2024 CKD Guideline (Kidney Int 2024;105(4S):S117)

---

## Definition

CKD = abnormalities of kidney structure or function present for > 3 months with health implications (KDIGO 2024).

Defined by either:
- GFR < 60 mL/min/1.73 m² for > 3 months, OR
- Kidney damage evidenced by >= 1 of: albuminuria (ACR >= 30 mg/g), urine sediment abnormalities, electrolyte abnormalities from tubular disorders, abnormal histology, structural abnormalities on imaging, history of kidney transplant

## KDIGO Staging (MUST MEMORIZE)

### GFR Categories

| Stage | GFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) | Description |
|---|---|---|
| G1 | >= 90 | Normal or high |
| G2 | 60-89 | Mildly decreased |
| G3a | 45-59 | Mild-to-moderately decreased |
| G3b | 30-44 | Moderate-to-severely decreased |
| G4 | 15-29 | Severely decreased |
| G5 | < 15 | Kidney failure (not on dialysis) |

**Exam pearl:** G1-G2 require evidence of kidney damage (albuminuria, structural abnormality) to qualify as CKD. GFR alone is insufficient for diagnosis at these stages.

### Albuminuria Categories

| Category | AER (mg/24h) | ACR (mg/g) | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| A1 | < 30 | < 30 (< 3 mg/mmol) | Normal to mildly increased |
| A2 | 30-299 | 30-299 (3-29 mg/mmol) | Moderately increased |
| A3 | >= 300 | >= 300 (>= 30 mg/mmol) | Severely increased (includes nephrotic) |

### KDIGO Risk Grid (GFR x Albuminuria)

| | A1 | A2 | A3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| G1 | Low | Moderate | High |
| G2 | Low | Moderate | High |
| G3a | Moderate | High | **Very high** |
| G3b | High | **Very high** | **Very high** |
| G4 | **Very high** | **Very high** | **Very high** |
| G5 | **Very high** | **Very high** | **Very high** |

**Exam pearl:** This grid determines monitoring frequency, referral criteria, and treatment intensity. Green = annual monitoring; Yellow = annual; Orange = q6 months; Red = q3 months.

## GFR Estimation

- Use creatinine-based eGFR for initial assessment [Strong Recommendation]
- If cystatin C available, use combination Cr + cystatin C equation [Strong Recommendation]
- Use CKD-EPI 2021 equation (race-free) [Strong Recommendation]
- Measured GFR (iohexol, inulin clearance) for: catabolic states, extremes of body size, transplant donor evaluation, drug dosing with narrow therapeutic index

**Exam pearl:** CKD-EPI 2021 replaced CKD-EPI 2009 and MDRD. The 2021 equation does NOT include race. Cystatin C is more accurate when muscle mass is unreliable (amputation, sarcopenia, obesity class 3).

---

## Referral Criteria

Refer to nephrology for:
- GFR < 30 mL/min/1.73 m² (always)
- ACR >= 300 mg/g or albuminuria >= 300 mg/24h
- Validated risk equation showing > 3-5% 5-year risk of kidney replacement therapy
- Sustained GFR fall > 20-30% after starting ACEi/ARB/SGLT2i
- Recurrent/extensive nephrolithiasis
- Hereditary kidney disease
- RBC casts or > 20 RBC/HPF unexplained
- HTN refractory to >= 4 agents
- Persistent K⁺, Na⁺, or acid-base abnormalities
- >= 2-fold increase in albuminuria

## Management -- Pharmacotherapy Hierarchy

### Blood Pressure

- Target < 130/80 mmHg [Strong Recommendation]
- Consider < 120 mmHg systolic using standardized office BP [Conditional Recommendation]
- KDIGO states < 120 mmHg SBP target is "potentially hazardous" if applied to nonstandardized measurements
- ACEi or ARB preferred first-line
- Continue ACEi/ARB unless serum Cr rises > 30% within 4 weeks, or symptomatic hypotension, or uncontrolled hyperkalemia

### RAAS Blockade

- ACEi or ARB in patients with:
  - A3 albuminuria (ACR >= 300) without diabetes [Strong Recommendation]
  - A2 albuminuria (ACR 30-299) without diabetes [Conditional Recommendation]
  - Diabetes + hypertension + A2-A3 albuminuria [Strong Recommendation]
- Titrate to highest approved/tolerated dose
- Do NOT combine ACEi + ARB + direct renin inhibitor [KDIGO Level 1, Grade B]
- Prescribe ARB or dihydropyridine CCB first-line in kidney transplant recipients [KDIGO Level 1, Grade B]

### SGLT2 Inhibitors

- Start SGLT2i in patients with:
  - T2DM + CKD with eGFR >= 20 mL/min/1.73 m² [Strong Recommendation]
  - eGFR >= 20 + urine ACR >= 200 mg/g, irrespective of albuminuria level [Strong Recommendation]
  - eGFR 20-45 + ACR < 200 mg/g [Conditional Recommendation]
  - Heart failure, irrespective of GFR [Strong Recommendation]
- Semaglutide (Ozempic) received expanded FDA approval for sustained eGFR decline reduction in T2DM + CKD
- Key trials:
  - **CREDENCE** (canagliflozin): 30% reduction in composite renal endpoint in T2DM + CKD
  - **DAPA-CKD** (dapagliflozin): 39% reduction in sustained GFR decline, ESKD, or renal death -- benefit regardless of diabetes status
  - **EMPA-KIDNEY** (empagliflozin): 28% reduction in CKD progression, extended to eGFR 20-45 without heavy albuminuria

### GLP-1 Receptor Agonists

- Offer long-acting GLP-1RA to patients not at glycemic target on metformin + SGLT2i [Strong Recommendation]

### Nonsteroidal MRA (Finerenone)

- Consider finerenone in T2DM + CKD with eGFR >= 25, normal K⁺, continued albuminuria (ACR > 30 mg/g) despite max ACEi/ARB [Conditional Recommendation]
- Key trials: FIDELIO-DKD, FIGARO-DKD

### Lipid Management

- Statin in adults >= 50 with CKD and eGFR >= 60 [Strong Recommendation]
- Statin or statin/ezetimibe if eGFR < 60 [Strong Recommendation]
- Do NOT initiate statin in dialysis patients (but continue if already on) [KDIGO Level 2, Grade A]

### Diabetes Management

- HbA1c target < 6.5-8% depending on patient factors [Strong Recommendation]
- Metformin: continue if eGFR >= 30, reduce dose if eGFR 30-45, stop if < 30
- HbA1c may be unreliable in advanced CKD (shortened RBC lifespan) -- consider glycated albumin or fructosamine

### Dietary

- Protein: moderate restriction (not recommended < 0.8 g/kg/day in non-dialysis CKD)
- Sodium: < 2 g/day [Strong Recommendation]
- Phosphate: limit to < 1500 mg/day in CKD 3-5

### Acid-Base

- Maintain serum HCO3⁻ >= 22 mmol/L with oral NaHCO3 [Conditional Recommendation]

## Cardiovascular Disease in CKD

### Key Facts

- CVD is the leading cause of morbidity and mortality in CKD
- Prevalence of CVD: 64.5% in all CKD patients (USRDS 2018)
- > 90% of ESKD patients have CVD
- CV event rate increases with declining GFR:
  - GFR 45-59: HR 1.4
  - GFR 30-44: HR 2.0
  - GFR 15-29: HR 2.8
  - GFR < 15: HR 3.4
  - (vs GFR >= 60, NEJM 2004)

### Cardiorenal Syndrome Classification

| Type | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| 1 | Acute cardiac dysfunction (e.g., decompensated HF) → AKI |
| 2 | Chronic cardiac dysfunction (e.g., chronic HF) → progressive CKD |
| 3 | Acute renal dysfunction (e.g., AKI, GN) → acute cardiac dysfunction |
| 4 | CKD → decreased cardiac function, LVH, ↑ CV events |
| 5 | Systemic condition (e.g., sepsis, diabetes) → both cardiac + renal dysfunction |

**Exam pearl:** Type 4 is the most common in nephrology practice. CKD causes LVH, vascular calcification, and accelerated atherosclerosis.

### CKD-Specific CV Risk Factors (Nontraditional)

- ↓ GFR itself
- Albuminuria
- Vascular calcification (intimal = atherosclerosis; medial = Monckeberg, CKD-specific)
- Anemia of CKD
- Chronic inflammation (↑ CRP, IL-6)
- Abnormal bone mineral metabolism (↑ FGF-23, ↑ PTH, ↑ phosphate)
- Hyperuricemia, hyperhomocysteinemia
- Oxidative stress
- Dialysis-related factors (type, frequency, dialysate composition)

### Vascular Calcification

- 50-90% of dialysis patients have vascular calcifications
- Intimal = atherosclerotic, driven by traditional risk factors
- Medial = CKD-specific, driven by hyperphosphatemia, ↑ Ca-P product, ↑ PTH
- Medial calcification → arterial stiffness → LVH → heart failure
- VSMC phenotypic switch to osteoblast-like cells is the pathogenic mechanism
- Inhibitors of calcification: matrix Gla protein (vitamin K dependent), fetuin-A, FGF-23/klotho, pyrophosphate

**Exam pearl:** Medial calcification is MORE SPECIFIC to CKD than intimal. It causes arterial stiffness (measured by pulse wave velocity) rather than luminal stenosis. This explains why CKD patients die of heart failure and arrhythmia more than acute MI.

### Management of CVD in CKD

- ACS: ACEi, ARBs, beta-blockers, antiplatelet therapy (same as general population)
- Aspirin: consider for CKD with eGFR < 45 but > 30 [AHA/ASA Class IIb]
- Atrial fibrillation: anticoagulation with dose adjustment for kidney function
- Heart failure: standard therapy applies; SGLT2i benefit extends to HFrEF/HFpEF in CKD
- Statins: continue in CKD (SHARP trial), but do NOT initiate in dialysis (4D, AURORA showed no benefit)

## Complications of CKD -- Quick Reference

| Complication | Onset (CKD stage) | Key intervention |
|---|---|---|
| Hypertension | G1-G2 | ACEi/ARB, Na restriction, BP < 130/80 |
| Anemia | G3-G4 (Hb check) | Iron first → ESA if needed |
| CKD-MBD | G3 (monitor) | See CKD-MBD entry |
| Metabolic acidosis | G4-G5 | NaHCO3 to maintain HCO3⁻ >= 22 |
| Hyperkalemia | G4-G5 | Diet, loop diuretics, K⁺ binders, avoid dual RAAS |
| Uremia | G5 | Dialysis initiation |
| Pruritus | G4-G5 | Gabapentin, difelikefalin, naltrexone; topical emollients |
| Gout | G3-G5 | Colchicine (dose adjust if GFR < 30), glucocorticoids preferred |

### Hyperkalemia in CKD

- Prevalence: 14-20% (K⁺ >= 5), severe (>= 5.5) in 3-8%
- Risk factors: ↓ GFR, ACEi/ARB use, male sex, DM, ↓ HCO3⁻
- Incidence increases sharply when GFR < 40 (from 2% to 42%)
- Both hyperkalemia AND hypokalemia are associated with ↑ mortality (U-shaped curve)
- Management: dietary K⁺ restriction, loop diuretics, patiromer/SZC for chronic management
- VA NEPHRON-D lesson: ACEi + ARB combination → ↑ hyperkalemia (9.9% vs 4.4%), ↑ AKI; trial stopped early

### Dialysis Initiation

- KDIGO 2024: initiate when signs/symptoms of uremia, volume overload, malnutrition, or changes in mental status, clinically appropriate -- often at eGFR 5-10 mL/min/1.73 m²
- IDEAL trial (NEJM 2010): early start (GFR 10-14) vs late (GFR 5-7) showed NO survival benefit → do not initiate based on GFR alone
- Plan dialysis access when GFR < 15-20 or KRT risk > 40% over 2 years

## High-Yield Exam Scenarios

1. **G3aA1 patient**: moderate risk, annual monitoring. No SGLT2i needed unless diabetic or heart failure.

2. **G3bA3 patient**: very high risk. Must be on ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i. Refer to nephrology. Monitor q3 months.

3. **Diabetic with eGFR 25, ACR 500**: Start SGLT2i (approved down to eGFR 20). Add finerenone if albuminuria persists despite max ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i. Statin therapy.

4. **CKD patient with Hb 9.5**: Check iron studies first. If TSAT < 20% and ferritin < 100 (non-dialysis) → iron repletion before ESA. Target Hb 10-12, do NOT exceed 13 (TREAT, CHOIR lessons).

5. **eGFR 8 with fatigue and nausea**: This is uremia at GFR 5-10 range. Initiate dialysis based on symptoms, not GFR cutoff. IDEAL trial showed no benefit to early start.

6. **Post-ACEi Cr rise of 25%**: Expected and acceptable. Continue therapy. Only stop if rise > 30% or hyperkalemia develops.

7. **CKD patient with acute coronary syndrome**: Treat same as general population (PCI, dual antiplatelet, statin). Adjust anticoagulant doses for GFR. CKD patients are LESS likely to present with typical chest pain.
