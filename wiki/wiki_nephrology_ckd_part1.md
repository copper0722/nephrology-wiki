# Nephrology Ckd — Part1 — Journal Reading Wiki

## Uses of GFR and Albuminuria Level in Acute and Chronic Kidney Disease (2022)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra2201153
Design: Review
Key: CGA staging (cause-GFR-albuminuria) replaces GFR-only classification; eGFRcr initial, eGFRcys/eGFRcr-cys for confirmation
Clinical: GFR and albuminuria are independent CVD predictors; guides surrogate endpoints for clinical trials
Numbers: GFR <60 or ACR >30 defines CKD/AKD threshold

## Congenital Hypermetabolism and Uncoupled Oxidative Phosphorylation (2022)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2202949
Design: Case report
Key: β-subunit Leu335Pro uncouples ATP synthase; euthyroid hypermetabolism 3× basal metabolic rate; phenocopies Luft syndrome
Clinical: Rare mitochondrial disorder; recognize hypermetabolism without hyperthyroidism
Numbers: Identical twins, GA 34 weeks; caloric intake 1280–1480 kcal (2.8–3.2× REE)

## Shared Decision-Making in Dialysis (2001)
DOI: 10.1053/ajkd.2001.24707
Design: Guideline commentary + case report
Key: RPA/ASN framework for dialysis initiation/withdrawal; focus on autonomy, symptom burden, quality of life
Clinical: Palliative care approach when medical complexity high; advance directives essential
Numbers: Case: 87-year-old with 6+ comorbidities chose comfort care, died peacefully

## Very-Low-Protein Diet in Elderly CKD G5 (2007)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2007.02.278
Design: RCT, prospective multicenter
Key: sVLPD (0.3 g/kg, vegan + keto-analogues) non-inferior to dialysis on 1-year mortality in elderly
Clinical: Dietary postponement option for selected frail patients; under-enrollment (112 vs 166)
Numbers: Age >70, GFR 5–7 ml/min, non-diabetic; mean follow-up 12 months

## Cinacalcet in CKD G3–G4 (Predialysis) (2008)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2008.09.021
Design: RCT, phase III, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Key: Calcimimetic lowers iPTH early; maintains normal calcium/phosphate; 404 patients CKD G3–4
Clinical: Early SHPT treatment may slow CKD progression; 32-week titration + 16-week efficacy phase
Numbers: iPTH ≥100 (G3) or ≥160 (G4) pg/mL; eGFR 15–59; 3:1 cinacalcet:placebo

## Early vs Late Dialysis Initiation—IDEAL Trial (2010)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa1000552
Design: RCT, 32 centers Australia/NZ
Key: Early (eGFR 10–14) vs late (5–7) initiation: no mortality difference; supports delayed start with symptom monitoring
Clinical: Refutes lead-time bias; contradicts observational data favoring early initiation; challenges routine GFR cutoffs
Numbers: Cockcroft-Gault eGFR 10.0–15.0 at entry; primary outcome all-cause mortality

## KDIGO Lipid Management in CKD (2013)
DOI: 10.1038/kisup.2013.31
Design: Guideline
Key: LDL-C <100 mg/dL for CKD G1–4; limited statin benefit for HD (AURORA, 4D trials); CVD risk stratification
Clinical: Dyslipidemia management differs by GFR stage and dialysis status; gaps in evidence for G5
Numbers: Incorporated ALERT, 4D, PREVEND IT, AURORA, SHARP trial evidence; updated from 2003 KDOQI

## Uremic Pericarditis: 30 Cases (2015)
DOI: 10.12659/AJCR.893140
Design: Case series + narrative review
Key: Rare uremic complication (<1% incidence now); pericardial friction rub underrecognized; may require pericardiocentesis/pericardiectomy
Clinical: High mortality if untreated; early recognition critical; common in pre-dialysis/underdialyzed patients
Numbers: 30 case retrospective review; historical incidence high, now rare due to dialysis adequacy

## Statins for CVD Primary Prevention in CKD (2015)
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.07460714
Design: Meta-analysis, systematic review
Key: Statin efficacy for primary CVD prevention in non-ESRD CKD modest; most benefit in G3; inconsistent across trials
Clinical: Primary care CKD (no existing CVD); limited evidence vs specialty-referred SHARP population
Numbers: 6 RCTs (AFCAPS, CARDS, JUPITER, MEGA, PREVEND IT, WOSCOPS); mortality RR 0.81

## Conservative Care of Elderly CKD (2016)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ackd.2015.08.003
Design: Narrative review + practice guide
Key: Renal supportive/palliative care for frail elderly; symptom management (pain, fatigue, pruritus, anorexia, dyspnea)
Clinical: Non-dialytic alternative; hospice for <6-month survival; advance care planning essential
Numbers: N/A (guideline framework)

## Sodium Bicarbonate for Metabolic Acidosis—UBI Study (2019)
DOI: 10.1007/s40620-019-00656-5
Design: RCT, pragmatic, 36-month, multicenter
Key: NaHCO₃ slows CKD progression in G3–5 with metabolic acidosis (HCO₃ 18–24); delays need for RRT
Clinical: Largest trial to date; improves muscle, bone outcomes; metabolic acidosis associates with CKD decline
Numbers: 134 patients; serum HCO₃ <22 mmol/L linked to eGFR decline >30%

## Timing & Modality of RRT in Pediatric ESRD (2019)
DOI: 10.1007/s00467-019-04391-8
Design: Cohort, retrospective
Key: Pediatric RRT driven by clinical symptoms + medical + psychosocial factors, not eGFR cutoffs; differs from adult IDEAL
Clinical: Infants start HD at lower eGFR; PD preferred <2 years; age/body size guide modality
Numbers: 95 children age 0–19 (2004–2014, Vancouver); varied start eGFR across age groups

## KDOQI Nutrition in CKD—2020 Update (2020)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2020.05.006
Design: Guideline
Key: Nutritional assessment, MNT, protein/energy/micronutrient/electrolyte guidance for CKD G1–5 + transplant
Clinical: Scope expanded beyond ESRD to all CKD stages; GRADE evidence synthesis; dietitian-driven approach
Numbers: N/A (guideline recommendations across 6 major domains)

## Dapagliflozin in CKD—Diabetes & Non-Diabetes Subanalysis (2020)
DOI: 10.1016/s2213-8587(20)30369-7
Design: RCT subanalysis, prespecified
Key: SGLT2i reduces eGFR decline, HF hospitalization in T2D + non-T2D CKD; benefits diabetes-independent
Clinical: Game-changer: efficacy in non-diabetic CKD; primary: eGFR ≥50% decline, ESRD, renal/CV death
Numbers: UACR 200–5000, eGFR 25–75; 1:1 dapagliflozin 10 mg daily vs placebo

## Bicarbonate Therapy for CKD Outcomes—Meta-analysis (2020)
DOI: 10.1016/j.ekir.2020.12.019
Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis
Key: NaHCO₃ modest benefit for eGFR preservation; metabolic acidosis <22 mmol/L linked to CKD progression
Clinical: KDIGO 2012 recommends oral HCO₃; concerns: sodium load, polypharmacy, cost-effectiveness
Numbers: CRIC cohort: HCO₃ +1 mmol/L → 3% ↓ renal failure/eGFR decline >50% risk

## Transitional Care Unit for Home Hemodialysis (2021)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ackd.2021.02.009
Design: Narrative review
Key: TCU bridges in-center HD → home modality choice; 30–50% choose home with education; <2% US HHD prevalence
Clinical: Improves patient activation, vascular access preparation; US goal 80% home/transplant by 2025
Numbers: <2% US utilization; 15% of home dialysis patients use HHD; only 12.5% home dialysis overall

## GFR Estimation Without Race—NKF-ASN Task Force (2021)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2021.08.003
Design: Special report
Key: Remove race coefficient from eGFR equations; health equity goal; addresses systemic racism in diagnosis
Clinical: CKD-EPI 2021 unified creatinine-cystatin approach; improves Black population risk assessment
Numbers: N/A (methodological/policy recommendation; affected millions US adults)

## Sodium Restriction & Albuminuria in Diabetic Kidney Disease (2021)
DOI: 10.1007/s11255-021-03035-x
Design: Meta-analysis
Key: Moderate Na restriction ↓ systolic BP 4.72 mmHg, diastolic 2.33 mmHg; AER ↓ 127.69 mg/min in macroalbuminuria
Clinical: Enhanced benefit macroalbuminuria vs microalbuminuria; limits RAAS overactivation with overrestriction
Numbers: 12 RCTs; albuminuria reduction greater in proteinuric CKD

## New GFR Equations Without Race (2021)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2102953
Design: Validation study + diagnostic equation development
Key: CKD-EPI 2021 creatinine-cystatin equations remove race; accurate for all groups without racial bias
Clinical: Prevents healthcare disparities; eGFRcr-cys most accurate; cystatin C reduces bias for Black patients
Numbers: Updated equations omit race; outperform prior race-adjusted versions across populations

## Phosphate-Lowering Agents in Non-Dialysis CKD (2021)
DOI: 10.1681/ASN.2021040554
Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis
Key: Non-calcium binders (sevelamer, lanthanum, ferric citrate) uncertain clinical benefit; limited RCT data non-dialysis
Clinical: Lack RCT evidence in pre-ESRD; KDIGO 2021 questions binder efficacy outside HD; constipation common side effect
Numbers: FGF23 elevation early CKD; multiple RCTs (IMPROVE-CKD, COMBINE, CRIB-Phos, others)

## Chlorthalidone for Hypertension in Advanced CKD (CLICK Trial) (2021)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2110730
Design: RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Key: Chlorthalidone effective CKD G4 (eGFR 23); ↓ 24-hour systolic BP 11 mmHg vs placebo; ACR ↓ 50%
Clinical: First evidence thiazide-like diuretic advanced CKD; hypokalemia, reversible Scr rise; 76% diabetic
Numbers: N=160; baseline eGFR 23±4; 3.4±1.4 concurrent antihypertensives; 12-week primary endpoint

## RAAS Inhibitor Discontinuation & CKD Outcomes (2022)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2022.01.002
Design: Cohort, population-based, retrospective
Key: Hyperkalemia-related RAAS inhibitor discontinuation → ↑ mortality, CV events, ESRD; benefits continuation outweigh hyperkalemia
Clinical: Most patients discontinue vs manage hyperkalemia; K-binders, diuretics needed as alternatives
Numbers: Manitoba + Ontario cohorts; K ≥5.5 mmol/L index event; propensity-score adjusted

## Dialysis Initiation Process—Guideline Recommendations (2022)
DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0266202
Design: Systematic review (AGREE II appraisal)
Key: CPG recommendations inconsistent on timing (eGFR 5–15), modality, shared decision-making; only IDEAL trial compares
Clinical: Most guides recommend individualized symptom-based approach; weak evidence for modality choice
Numbers: CKD affects >10% adults; ~3M dialysis patients globally; only 11% on PD

## Non-Steroidal MRAs in Cardiorenal Disease (2022)
DOI: 10.1093/eurheartj/ehac299
Design: Narrative review
Key: Finerenone, esaxerenone reduce adverse kidney/CV events T2D+CKD; anti-fibrotic without hyperkalemia vs steroidal MRAs
Clinical: FIGARO-DKD, FIDELIO-DKD landmark trials; synergy with SGLT2i; esaxerenone approved Japan; finerenone global
Numbers: Finerenone approved FDA; esaxerenone Japan; both show eGFR decline benefit

## KDIGO 2022 Diabetes Management in CKD (2022)
DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2022.06.008
Design: Guideline
Key: SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA first-line T2D+CKD (↓ eGFR decline, CV/HF events); 2020 guideline updated with new RCT evidence
Clinical: 40%+ diabetics develop CKD; new agents game-changers; rapid evidence evolution required guideline update
Numbers: 5.37B diabetics globally (2021); projected 7.84B by 2045; 40%+ develop CKD

## IgAN Targeted Therapy: VISIONARY (Sibeprenlimab) & ORIGIN 3 (Atacicept) (kb/nephrology)

Source: kb/nephrology/VISIONARY（sibeprenlimab）與ORIGIN 3（atacicept）期中分析 - IgAN.md + proj/wiki/igan_targeted_therapy.md

- **VISIONARY (sibeprenlimab)**: Phase 3, anti-APRIL mAb. 24-week interim: proteinuria reduction ~50% vs placebo; favorable safety.
- **ORIGIN 3 (atacicept)**: Phase 3, TACI-Fc (blocks APRIL + BLyS). Interim: significant proteinuria reduction; eGFR slope benefit emerging.
- Both target the APRIL/BLyS-IgA axis upstream of Gd-IgA1 production.
- Comparison: different mechanisms (APRIL-only vs dual APRIL/BLyS), similar magnitude of proteinuria benefit at interim.
- Context: sparsentan (endothelin+RAS), iptacopan (complement factor B) = alternative pathways under investigation.
