# Nephrology Ckd — Part2 — Journal Reading Wiki

## GFR Estimation in Preemptive Kidney Transplant Listing (2022)
DOI: 10.1016/j.ekir.2022.12.021
Design: Cohort study
Key: Race-adjusted eGFR equations delay preemptive listing for Black candidates by inflating measured GFR; race-neutral equations improve equity in transplant access.
Clinical: Standardize eGFR reporting to eliminate racial coefficients affecting candidacy.
Numbers: Disparities in preemptive listing persist across U.S. centers; 2022 OPTN policy mandated race-free eGFR equations.

## Magnesium & Vascular Calcification in CKD (2023)
DOI: 10.1080/0886022X.2023.2182603
Design: Systematic review and meta-analysis
Key: Magnesium supplementation may attenuate vascular calcification in CKD; mechanisms include blocking hydroxyapatite crystal formation and inhibiting Wnt/β-catenin signaling.
Clinical: Emerging data support Mg as adjunctive therapy in CKD-MBD; further trials needed.
Numbers: N/A

## Preemptive Living Donor Kidney Transplant (2023)
DOI: 10.3389/fpubh.2023.1124453
Design: Opinion piece
Key: Preemptive LDKT should be default KRT pathway; superior graft/patient survival, avoids dialysis morbidity, enables kidney exchange chains.
Clinical: Policy shift needed to increase PLDKT rates—currently <20% globally despite strong outcome evidence.
Numbers: PKT vs nPKT: 5-year graft survival ~60% vs ~45%.

## Hypoglycemia in Advanced CKD via CGM (2023)
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.0000000000000102
Design: Cross-sectional study
Key: Undetected hypoglycemia common in advanced CKD; CGM reveals ≥10× higher hypoglycemia frequency in CKD G4–G5D vs non-CKD diabetics.
Clinical: CGM warranted to guide glucose targets in CKD; avoid intensive glucose control.
Numbers: Hypoglycemia episodes/week: CKD G5D ~2–3 vs non-CKD DM ~0.1–0.3.

## KDIGO 2024 CKD Clinical Practice Guideline (2024)
DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2023.10.018
Design: Guideline
Key: Updated KDIGO CKD assessment & management for adults & children; integrated four-pillar therapy (RASi, SGLT2i, ns-MRA, GLP-1 RA) for CV/kidney risk reduction.
Clinical: Standard reference for CKD diagnosis, staging, progression monitoring, complication management.
Numbers: N/A

## SGLT-2 Inhibitors for Adult CKD (2024)
DOI: 10.1136/bmj-2024-080257
Design: Clinical practice guideline
Key: SGLT-2i recommended for all CKD adults (with or without T2DM) stratified by CKD progression/complication risk; strong evidence for high/very-high risk groups.
Clinical: Universal recommendation across CKD stages G1–G4 with albuminuria or eGFR <30.
Numbers: 5-year NNT ~10–20 for high-risk groups to prevent ESRD/CV death.

## Phosphorus Metabolism in CKD (2024)
DOI: 10.1111/nep.14407
Design: Narrative review
Key: Phosphate-centered hypothesis in CKD-MBD; FGF23, PTH, klotho regulate phosphate balance; disruption drives vascular calcification & bone disease.
Clinical: Target-driven phosphate management essential; focus on lowering FGF23 via phosphate binders, vitamin D, calcimimetics.
Numbers: Serum P normal range 3.4–4.5 mg/dL; circadian variation important for interpretation.

## GLP-1 RA Effects on DKD (2024)
DOI: 10.7759/cureus.71739
Design: Systematic review and meta-analysis
Key: GLP-1 RAs reduce albuminuria & slow eGFR decline in T2DM; benefits extend beyond glucose control.
Clinical: Recommend GLP-1 RA as add-on therapy in T2DM with CKD or albuminuria; comparable efficacy to SGLT2i.
Numbers: Meta-analysis: eGFR change −0.5 to −1.5 mL/min/1.73m² annually with GLP-1 RA vs placebo.

## Win Ratio for Composite Outcomes in Nephrology Trials (2024)
DOI: 10.1681/ASN.0000000597
Design: Commentary
Key: Win ratio analysis accounts for hierarchical clinical importance of composite endpoints (e.g., CV death > ESRD > 50% eGFR decline); superior to Cox time-to-first-event model.
Clinical: Improves interpretation of SGLT2i & other CKD trials; captures non-fatal recurrent events & soft endpoints.
Numbers: N/A

## Cardiac Troponins in Kidney Disease (2025)
DOI: 10.15420/ecr.2024.51
Design: Narrative review
Key: Baseline cTn elevated in CKD due to reduced renal clearance; cTn kinetics altered—prognostic value preserved but diagnostic thresholds differ.
Clinical: Interpret cTn cautiously in CKD; eGFR-stratified cutoffs needed; elevated baseline ≠ myocardial injury alone.
Numbers: N/A

## Sevelamer Crystal-Induced GI Injury (2025)
DOI: 10.1053/j.jrn.2025.01.008
Design: Case series and literature review
Key: Sevelamer crystallization causes rare but serious GI mucosal injury; recognize "fish-scale" crystals on endoscopy.
Clinical: Consider alternative phosphate binders if GI symptoms emerge; ensure adequate hydration.
Numbers: N/A (rare complication; case-based evidence).

## P2X7R Purinergic Signaling in Kidney Disease (2025)
DOI: 10.1681/ASN.0000000711
Design: Narrative review
Key: Extracellular ATP–P2X7R axis drives NLRP3 inflammasome activation in AKI, CKD, & transplant rejection; emerging therapeutic target.
Clinical: P2X7R antagonists experimental; potential role in reducing chronic inflammation in CKD.
Numbers: N/A

## Late Allograft Loss & Cardiorenal Metabolic Therapies (2025)
DOI: 10.1681/ASN.0000000726
Design: Narrative review
Key: Late graft loss ~50% from function decline (death-censored) & ~50% from CV death with graft function; SGLT2i, ns-MRA, GLP-1 RA may benefit transplant recipients.
Clinical: Emerging evidence supports four-pillar therapy in kidney transplant recipients; off-label use currently.
Numbers: N/A

## Gut-Derived Uremic Toxins in CKD (2025)
DOI: 10.4103/tcmj.tcmj_293_24
Design: Narrative review
Key: p-Cresol, indoxyl sulfate, TMAO accumulate in CKD; promote endothelial dysfunction & arterial stiffness via microbiota dysbiosis.
Clinical: Dietary protein modification, prebiotics, & AST-120 may reduce UT burden; larger trials needed.
Numbers: IS, pCS, PAGln levels correlate with CV risk in CKD.

## Rapid & Simultaneous Initiation of Four-Pillar Therapy (2025)
DOI: 10.1681/ASN.0000000752
Design: Narrative review
Key: Rapid sequential or simultaneous RASi, SGLT2i, ns-MRA, GLP-1 RA initiation can accelerate CV/kidney benefit; safety data emerging.
Clinical: Consider expedited titration schedules; monitor K+, eGFR, BP closely; most evidence from single-drug trials.
Numbers: N/A

Key: Iliac bone biopsy remains gold standard for ROD diagnosis; adopt TMV classification; lack of standardized reference values hampers diagnosis.
Clinical: Recommend external quality control & standardization of biopsy protocols across centers.
Numbers: N/A

## Decongestion in Advanced CKD with Heart Failure (2025)
DOI: 10.1093/ckj/sfaf185
Design: Narrative review
Key: Congestion cascades from hemodynamic (asymptomatic) to clinical pulmonary edema; diuretics + SGLT2i + ultrafiltration core strategies.
Clinical: Monitor B-lines (lung ultrasound) & BNP; individualize salt/fluid restriction; SGLT2i improves diuresis in CKD–HF overlap.
Numbers: N/A

## Preventing CKD & Maintaining Kidney Health (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2025.04.005
Design: KDIGO Controversies Conference report
Key: CKD prevention neglected; CV-kidney-metabolic framework; SGLT2i & GLP-1 RA may regress T2DM/obesity-associated CKD.
Clinical: Shift paradigm toward primary prevention; lifespan risk assessment + targeted intervention needed.
Numbers: N/A

## APOL1 Kidney Disease (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2025.05.017
Design: KDIGO Controversies Conference report
Key: APOL1 G1/G2 risk variants increase CKD/FSGS risk in African ancestry; terminology "APOL1 kidney disease" adopted; routine screening not yet recommended.
Clinical: Genotyping valuable for risk stratification & clinical trial enrollment; no approved targeted therapy yet.
Numbers: APOL1 high-risk genotype confers 2–10-fold increased CKD risk depending on phenotype.

## Eryptosis in CKD & Dialysis (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/cells14130967
Design: Narrative review
Key: Programmed RBC death accelerated in CKD via uremic toxins, oxidative stress, inflammation; contributes to renal anemia.
Clinical: Anemia management may require addressing eryptosis beyond EPO/iron supplementation.
Numbers: N/A

## SGLT-2i in Non-Diabetic CKD (2025)
DOI: 10.3389/fmed.2025.1574693
Design: Narrative review
Key: SGLT-2i reduce albuminuria, delay eGFR decline via tubuloglomerular feedback & anti-inflammatory mechanisms in non-DM CKD.
Clinical: DAPA-CKD, CREDENCE, EMPA-KIDNEY trials support universal use across all CKD etiologies.
Numbers: ~30% ESRD/CV death reduction over 2–3 years.

## Parathyroidectomy for CKD Hyperparathyroidism (2025)
DOI: 10.58483/nsap.00252025
Design: Narrative review and editorial
Key: Secondary HPT common in CKD; surgery reserved for drug-refractory cases; calcimimetics now standard medical therapy.
Clinical: Parathyroidectomy consideration when PTH >10× upper limit normal + symptoms despite pharmacotherapy; lack of RCT guidance.
Numbers: <1% dialysis patients undergo parathyroidectomy despite CKD-MBD prevalence.

## Fracture Risk in CKD (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/metabo15070460
Design: Narrative review
Key: 18–32% CKD patients have osteoporosis; fracture risk 2.5–4× general population; overlap of CKD-MBD & osteoporosis increases fragility.
Clinical: Bone biopsy + BMD/TBS recommended for G4–G5; denosumab, teriparatide, romosozumab emerging therapies.
Numbers: Hip fracture mortality 2.4× higher in dialysis vs non-CKD.

## Cardiovascular Mortality in Hemodialysis (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/toxins17070345
Design: Narrative review
Key: HD-associated chronic inflammation, complement activation, NETs, CARPA link non-biocompatible dialysis membranes to excess CV death.
Clinical: Consider high-biocompatibility membranes; synthetic vs cellulose choice affects CVD risk.
Numbers: HD CV mortality >2× peritoneal dialysis.

Key: CKD dysbiosis (↓ SCFA-producers, ↑ pathogens) drives UT production & intestinal barrier breakdown; KRT modality affects microbiota composition.
Clinical: Probiotics, prebiotics, plant-based diet, FMT emerging interventions; personalized microbiota-targeting therapy promising.
