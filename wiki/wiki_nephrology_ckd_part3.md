# Nephrology Ckd — Part3 — Journal Reading Wiki

## Management of osteoporosis in chronic kidney disease (2025)
DOI: 10.1093/ckj/sfaf228
Design: Narrative review
Key: CKD patients face elevated fracture risk from mineral-bone disorders; early diagnosis and integrated management reduces burden.
Clinical: CKD-related osteoporosis underdiagnosed; requires tailored approach distinct from standard protocols.
Numbers: N/A

## Preclinical approaches in regenerative medicine for ESRD (2025)
DOI: 10.3389/fendo.2025.1549744
Design: Scoping review
Key: Kidney organoids and organ-on-chip platforms most translational; stem cell/tissue engineering offer functional kidney replacement.
Clinical: Regenerative approaches address dialysis/transplant limitations; long-term ESRD solution pathway.
Numbers: 111 studies included (of 5,869 records screened)

## Patient preferences for kidney failure treatments (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2025.05.035
Design: Narrative review (mini-review)
Key: Person-centered care requires shared decision-making; best treatment aligns with individual values and preferences.
Clinical: Preference-sensitive approach essential for adherence and outcomes; transforms renal care culture.
Numbers: N/A

## Breaking barriers: Neprilysin inhibition in chronic cardiorenal syndrome (2025)
DOI: 10.4330/wjc.v17.i7.107539
Design: Narrative review
Key: ARNI effective for HF in advanced CKD/ESKD despite prior trial exclusions; expands cardio-renal protective options.
Clinical: Addresses unmet HF-CKD need; ~50% HF patients have comorbid CKD.
Numbers: 47% US adults hypertensive; 38% dyslipidemic

## Repurposing diabetes therapies in CKD: SGLT2i and GLP-1RA (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/ph18081130
Design: Narrative review
Key: SGLT2i + GLP-1RA combined reduce inflammation/fibrosis/oxidative stress; landmark trials (CREDENCE/DAPA-CKD/EMPA-KIDNEY) show 25–40% RRR kidney failure.
Clinical: Dual therapy paradigm toward cardiorenal-metabolic integration; requires mechanism-driven individualization.
Numbers: CKD affects >700M (9% population); DKD 50% CKD cases; ~40% T2DM develop DKD

## Electrophysiology models for ESRD asystole promotion (2025)
DOI: 10.31083/RCM37400
Design: Narrative review
Key: ESRD remodeling + hyperkalemia/pericarditis impair cardiac conduction; animal models reveal asystole mechanisms.
Clinical: Electrophysiologic complications common in dialysis ESRD; targeted monitoring may reduce sudden death.
Numbers: eGFR <15 mL/min/1.73m² defines ESRD; high electrophysiologic mortality vs normal kidneys

## Advances in epigenetic mechanisms of diabetic nephropathy (2025)
DOI: 10.2147/DMSO.567171
Design: Narrative review
Key: DNA methylation, histone modifications, miRNAs regulate DN inflammation/fibrosis; epigenetics reveal diagnostic/therapeutic targets.
Clinical: Personalized epigenetic approaches promise improved DN prevention beyond metabolic interventions.
Numbers: ~40% diabetics develop DN; 529M diabetics globally (2021), projected 1.3B by 2050

## SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 agonists in CKM syndrome (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/biomedicines13081924
Design: Narrative review
Key: CKM unified pathophysiology; SGLT2i/GLP-1RA provide synergistic multi-organ protection; early dual therapy transforms care.
Clinical: Patient-centered integration paradigm; cost/accessibility barriers limit current use.
Numbers: ~40% T2DM develop CKD; CKD patients 2–4× more likely CV death than ESRD progression

## Childhood-onset lupus nephritis: management uniqueness (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2025.05.039
Design: Mini-review
Key: cLN more severe than adult LN (higher relapse, lower remission); genetic factors significant; newer agents underutilized in pediatrics.
Clinical: Requires developmental immunology perspective; treatment adherence + transition care critical for outcomes.
Numbers: 10–20% SLE childhood-onset; 40–60% pediatric SLE develop LN; 22× mortality vs healthy children

## Diabetes and the kidney (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.clinme.2025.100512
Design: Narrative review
Key: DKD (preferred term) management standardized: glycemic control, BP, RAASi, lipid, smoking + kidney failure risk equations.
Clinical: Five-finger rule ensures comprehensive care; sick-day rules prevent medication-induced progression.
Numbers: ~33% diabetics have CKD; heterogeneous phenotypes (aging, HTN, obesity cofactors)

## Gut microbiome-derived SCFAs in glomerular protection (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/nu17172904
Design: Narrative review
Key: SCFAs (acetate, propionate, butyrate) via GPR41/43/109A protect glomerulus; dysbiosis reduces SCFA producers, increases uremic toxins.
Clinical: Gut-kidney axis targeting via SCFA restoration or prebiotic/probiotic strategies offers CKD therapeutic opportunity.
Numbers: CKD affects ~700M (10–15% population); WHO ranks kidney disease 9th leading death cause (doubled 2000–2021)

## Chronic kidney disease (2025)
DOI: 10.7326/ANNALS-25-02684
Design: Narrative review (In the Clinic)
Key: KDIGO-recommended screening for high-risk groups; SGLT2i effective across all eGFR/albuminuria; modern agents target CV-renal-metabolic endpoints.
Clinical: Person-centered risk equations guide screening intensity; SGLT2i/GLP-1RA/finerenone reduce progression across phenotypes.
Numbers: ~14% US adults have CKD

## Osteoporosis (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(25)01385-6
Design: Narrative review (Lancet Seminar)
Key: BMD screening + FRAX risk assessment guide antiresorptive/anabolic therapy; special populations (elderly, CKD, fracture-resistant) require nuanced management.
Clinical: Denosumab discontinuation protocol prevents rebound fractures; SGLT2i/GLP-1RA may preserve bone in CKD-MBD.
Numbers: N/A

## Finerenone: evidence for benefit in early CKM syndrome (2025)
DOI: 10.1159/000548494
Design: Narrative review
Key: FIDELIO-DKD/FIGARO-DKD show finerenone reduces kidney failure/CV outcomes in CKD+T2DM; FINEARTS-HF prevents albuminuric CKD.
Clinical: Non-steroidal MRA paradigm shift toward early intervention in albuminuric CKD despite eGFR >60; multi-agent regimens optimal.
Numbers: CKD ranks 10th leading death cause (projected 5th by 2050)

## Lupus nephritis (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41572-025-00653-y
Design: Narrative review (Nature Reviews Disease Primers)
Key: LN affects 25–60% SLE; type I IFN, calcineurin, B/T cell dysfunction drive disease; multi-agent regimens achieve remission.
Clinical: Multidisciplinary care addresses immune + non-immune factors; B-cell therapies reduce flares and slow GFR decline.
Numbers: 5–20% childhood LN progress to ESRD; recurrent activity strongest CKD→ESRD predictor

## Microbiota and kidney disease: the road ahead (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41581-025-00988-5
Design: Narrative review (Roadmap)
Key: Bidirectional gut-kidney axis: dysbiosis promotes uremic toxins, impairs SCFA; microbiota therapeutics show preclinical promise.
Clinical: Microbiota biomarkers may identify AKI/CKD/stone disease risk; probiotics/prebiotics/metabolite restoration emerging frontier.
Numbers: >850M globally have CKD (10–15% population)

## Pannexin channels in the kidney (2025)
DOI: 10.1152/ajprenal.00259.2025
Design: Narrative review
Key: Pannexins (PANX1/PANX3) mediate ATP release, purinergic signaling; dysfunction implicated in HTN, AKI, ADPKD.
Clinical: Pannexin modulation may prevent hypertension-associated CKD and ADPKD progression; requires validation.
Numbers: N/A

## Optimizing hemodialysis using bioelectrical impedance analysis (2025)
DOI: 10.1080/0886022X.2025.2567037
Design: Narrative review
Key: BIA phase angle reflects cell integrity, correlates with nutritional status/mortality; guides individualized dry-weight management.
Clinical: Non-invasive BIA predicts sarcopenia/frailty/ESA resistance; improves personalized dialysis prescription.
Numbers: 492,987 US HD patients (2020); 83.6% Korean dialysis on HD; severe fluid overload = 4.2× mortality risk

## Disparities in kidney disease and race-neutral eGFR (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMp2512030
Design: Perspective
Key: Race-adjusted eGFR masked CKD in Black patients, reduced transplant access; 2021 race-neutral algorithm reveals prior underdiagnosis.
Clinical: Safety-net hospitals without transplant programs require coordinated advocacy for transplant equity.
Numbers: Implemented system-wide across 11 acute care + 60 ambulatory NYC H+H centers (2022)

## SGLT2 inhibitors and kidney outcomes by GFR and albuminuria (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.20834
Design: Meta-analysis
Key: SGLT2i reduce CKD progression (25–40% RRR) across all eGFR categories including stage 4; benefit extends to non-albuminuric CKD.
Clinical: SGLT2i guideline recommendations should remove eGFR/albuminuria thresholds; more universal early use justified.
Numbers: Pooled evidence: canagliflozin, dapagliflozin, empagliflozin; consistent benefit eGFR <30–≥60 mL/min/1.73m²

## Effects of SGLT2 inhibitors by diabetes and albuminuria status (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.20835
Design: Meta-analysis
Key: SGLT2i benefit CKD progression/CV outcomes in both diabetic and non-diabetic CKD regardless of albuminuria level (UACR ≥200 or <200 mg/g).
Clinical: Broader CKD populations derive absolute benefit; guideline albuminuria stratification should be removed.
Numbers: 58,816 participants across 8 RCTs (SMART-C consortium); UACR ≥200 mg/g = higher baseline risk, larger absolute renal benefit

## Discordance in creatinine- and cystatin C-based eGFR (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.17578
Design: Meta-analysis
Key: eGFRcys significantly lower than eGFRcr in 11% outpatients/35% inpatients; large negative discordance (≥30%) predicts higher CV/kidney death risk.
Clinical: Dual eGFR markers improve risk stratification; cystatin C unmasks hidden CKD in muscle-depleted populations.
Numbers: 821,327 outpatients, 39,639 inpatients; large discordance = HR 1.69 all-cause mortality, HR 1.48 ESRD progression

## From microscopic hematuria to kidney failure: IgA nephropathy progression (2025)
DOI: 10.31128/AJGP-01-25-7536
Design: Narrative review + case reports
Key: IgAN most common primary GN globally; 1/3 progress to ESRD within 20 years; primary care detection of hematuria/proteinuria triggers early nephro referral.
Clinical: GP role in identifying high-risk features (progressive hematuria, HTN) essential; ARB + SGLT2i now standard induction.
Numbers: IgAN affects 200,000–350,000 annually; ~1/3 develop kidney failure within 20 years

## Low-protein diet for CKD: evidence, controversies, practical guidelines (2025)
DOI: 10.1111/joim.20117
Design: Narrative review (Perspective)
Key: LPD (0.6 g/kg/day) reduces urea, inflammation, fibrosis when monitored; excess protein linked to CVD, phosphate dysregulation, metabolic acidosis.
Clinical: Individualized protein restriction with malnutrition surveillance appropriate for non-dialysis CKD; balance nephroprotection vs muscle preservation.
Numbers: RDA 0.8 g/kg/day; DM patients target 0.6–0.8 g/kg/day; KDIGO cautions LPD malnutrition risk

## Obesity and kidney disease: focus on ciliopathies (2025)
DOI: 10.69097/42-04-2025-07
Design: Narrative review
Key: Obesity independent CKD risk (RR 1.51 albuminuria, RR 1.28 eGFR <60); accelerates ADPKD; GLP-1RA show renoprotection independent weight loss.
Clinical: New anti-obesity agents offer renal protection beyond metabolic benefits; combined lifestyle + pharmacotherapy optimal.
Numbers: 4–10% obese patients have proteinuria; obesity predisposes nephrolithiasis

## Calcimimetics for sHPT in CKD G5D (Negri 2024)
citationKey: Negri2024Calcimimetics
Design: Narrative review comparing 4 calcimimetics
Authors: Negri AL, Bover J, Vervloet M, Cozzolino M

**Background**: sHPT incidence rises from 20% in CKD G3a to >80% in G5. Severely elevated PTH associated with increased total mortality and CVD. CaSR is principal regulator of PTH secretion.

**4 Calcimimetics comparison:**

| Drug | Route | Approval | Bioavailability | Renal/HD clearance | GI side effects | CYP2D6 inhibition | Key trial result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cinacalcet** | PO once daily | FDA 2004 | 5.1-28.4% | N/A | Nausea 31%, vomiting 27% | **Strong** inhibitor | PTH ↓43% vs placebo +9% (P<0.001). EVOLVE (N=3883): NS primary composite; sig ↓ parathyroidectomy, calciphylaxis |
| **Etelcalcetide** | IV post-HD 3x/wk | 2016-2017 | N/A (IV) | ~60% removed by HD | Nausea 10.7%, hypocalcemia 7.0% | None | ≥30% PTH reduction: 74-75% vs 8-10% placebo (P=0.001) |
| **Evocalcet** | PO once daily | Japan 2018 | **62.7%** | N/A | **18.6%** (half of cinacalcet 32.8%, P<0.001) | **Decreased** | Non-inferior to cinacalcet: 72.7% vs 76.7% iPTH target |
| **Upacicalcet** | IV post-HD | Japan 2021 | N/A (IV) | **~80% removed by HD** | **Similar to placebo** | N/A | iPTH target: 67% vs 8% placebo; no gastric emptying effect |

**Key structural modifications (cinacalcet → evocalcet)**:
1. Pyrrolidine ring replaces linear alkyl chain → reduced CYP2D6 inhibition
2. Carboxylic acid moiety replaces trifluoromethyl → improved BA from 5-28% to 62.7%

**Upacicalcet advantages**: non-peptidic injectable; CaSR activation calcium-dependent (no agonism below physiological Ca → less hypocalcemia risk); no gastric emptying effect even at 300x effective dose; 80% HD removal prevents accumulation.

**Gaps**: no head-to-head RCTs between new and old calcimimetics; evocalcet and upacicalcet only approved in Japan.
