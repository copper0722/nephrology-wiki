Design: Narrative review

## Expanded Hemodialysis: Multidimensional Impact (2025)
DOI: 10.1111/sdi.13257
Design: Comprehensive review
Key: HDx (MCO membranes) improves β₂-microglobulin and large-solute clearance; reduces inflammatory markers and CVD biomarkers; improves QoL vs conventional HD.
Clinical: Better solute profile and patient outcomes; albumin loss minimal and clinically acceptable; infrastructure-compatible, cost-effective.
Numbers: Enhanced β₂-microglobulin removal; shorter recovery time; reduced PRO-BNP/inflammatory cytokines.

## Case 17-2025: Kidney Transplant with Respiratory Failure and Shock (2025)

Design: Case report/clinical conference
Key: Immunosuppressed transplant recipient developed donor-transmitted parasitic infection (eosinophilia 550/μL key clue) with acute kidney injury, fluid overload, GI symptoms at 10 weeks post-transplant.
Clinical: Rare donor-derived infections in immunosuppressed hosts; eosinophilia guides differential; emphasize donor screening and parasite prophylaxis.

## Why Is Glomerular Filtration Rate So High? (2020)
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.14300920
Design: Narrative review
Key: High GFR evolved for metabolic waste clearance (scales with metabolic rate, not volume alone); multimodal clearance (secretion + protein-binding + filtration) prevents uremic toxin accumulation.
Clinical: Explains uremia pathophysiology; informs artificial kidney design requirements.
Numbers: ~20% cardiac output to kidneys; ~10% resting energy for reabsorption; GFR ∝ metabolic rate across species.

## Lupus Nephritis (2025)
DOI: 10.58483/nsap.2025.24.1
Design: Clinical review/educational article
Key: LN progresses through 4 stages (tolerance loss → IC deposition → active inflammation → fibrosis); new immunosuppressive options enable individualized treatment; biomarker-driven phenotyping and damage prevention remain unmet needs.
Clinical: ~30% untreated LN progresses to ESRD in 15 years; combination therapy now standard; identify high-risk phenotypes for intensive intervention.
Numbers: >6,500 SLE patients, 12,500 controls in genetic study; 30% progression to ESRD (15-year untreated).

## Xenotransplantation: Long Awaited, Much Learned (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMe2504399
Design: Editorial
Key: Genetically-modified pig kidney (10 genetic mods, PERV-inactivated) functioned 52 days in immunosuppressed recipient; recipient died from coronary disease, not rejection; raises questions on pig engineering strategy, immunosuppression intensity, and recipient selection ethics.
Clinical: Potential organ shortage solution; early function encouraging but long-term safety/efficacy unproven; careful recipient selection and ethics review essential.
Numbers: 6 FDA-approved xenotransplants as of Feb 2025 (2 heart, 4 kidney); 52-day graft survival; patient age 62 with DM nephropathy.
