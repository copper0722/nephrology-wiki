# Nephrology Dialysis Hd — Journal Reading Wiki

## Equivalent Renal Urea Clearance (1996)
DOI: 10.1093/ndt/11.8.1574
Design: Kinetic modeling + clinical validation
Key: Proposes EKRc parameter standardizing dialysis adequacy assessment across all HD schedules; EKRc≥11 ml/min for standard dosing
Clinical: Unified adequacy target independent of treatment type/frequency
Numbers: 66 HD patients; linear relationship between EKRc and Kt/V

## HEMO Study: Dialysis Dose and Membrane Flux (2002)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa021583
Design: RCT, 2×2 factorial
Key: 1,846 patients randomized; higher dose (Kt/V 1.71 vs 1.32) and high flux failed to reduce mortality or major morbidity
Clinical: Challenges assumption that increased Kt/V improves outcomes
Numbers: HR 0.96 (95% CI 0.84–1.10) for mortality; HR 0.92 for high flux

## Solute-Solver Web Tool (2009)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2009.06.033
Design: Software development/validation
Key: Open-source JavaScript tool for batch urea kinetics calculations; supports variable schedules (daily, nocturnal, conventional)
Clinical: Facilitates practitioner dosing assessment across diverse regimens
Numbers: Processes 300 patients in 30–60 seconds

## FHN Daily Trial: Six vs Three Times Weekly (2010)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa1001593
Design: RCT, multicenter, 120 conventional vs 120 twice-weekly HD
Key: Frequent HD improves LV mass and phosphate control; modest QoL gains; no major outcome advantage
Clinical: Targeted benefit in surrogate endpoints; patient-centered approach needed
Numbers: N=240; LV mass reduction and better phosphate control in frequent arm

## Incremental HD and Residual Kidney Function (2016)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2016.01.008
Design: Longitudinal cohort, 23,645 incident dialysis patients
Key: Incremental HD preserves RKF 16% and urine volume 15%; mortality neutral in high RKF but HR 1.61 in low RKF
Clinical: Suitable for preserved RKF; high-frequency essential if RKF declining
Numbers: 351 incremental vs 8,068 matched conventional; RKF preservation 16%

## Incremental HD Initiation: Systematic Review (2022)
DOI: 10.1093/ndt/gfac274
Design: Systematic review + meta-analysis (22 cohorts + 2 RCTs, N=101,476)
Key: Gradual HD initiation neutral on mortality (HR 0.99) and lower admission (RR 0.31 in RCTs); cost-effective
Clinical: Safe alternative preserving RKF with reduced hospitalizations
Numbers: HR 0.99 (95% CI 0.80–1.24) for mortality

## Vascular Access Inflow-Outflow Imbalance (2024)
DOI: 10.1177/11297298241272166
Design: Narrative review
Key: Venous stenosis diagnostic criteria (clinical, ultrasound, hemodynamic); bimodal management (angioplasty vs flow reduction)
Clinical: Early identification prevents thrombosis and improves access longevity
Numbers: N/A

## Medium Cut-Off Membranes in HD (2025)
DOI: 10.3904/kjim.2025.049
Design: Comprehensive narrative review
Key: MCO membranes superior to high-flux in large middle-molecule removal while preserving albumin; improves QoL
Clinical: Enhanced uremic toxin removal without protein loss; ESA dose reduction
Numbers: N/A

## Oxylanthanum Carbonate Phase 2 Trial (2025)
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.0000000780
Design: Phase 2 clinical trial
Key: Novel lantanum-based phosphate binder (500 mg tablet, 12 mm diameter, swallowable whole); maintains serum phosphate ≤5.5 mg/dl
Clinical: Reduces pill burden; improved adherence vs lanthanum carbonate
Numbers: Target sP ≤5.5 mg/dl; tablet volume 0.35 cm³ vs 1.33 cm³ for comparator

## Acute Salicylate Toxicity (2025)
DOI: 10.7759/cureus.91505
Design: Narrative review
Key: Salicylate uncouples oxidative phosphorylation; causes respiratory alkalosis + anion gap metabolic acidosis; HD indicated for severe toxicity
Clinical: HD critical life-saving measure in acute poisoning
Numbers: N/A

## Intermittent Infusion Hemodiafiltration (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/toxins17090442
Design: Narrative review
Key: I-HDF combines HD with periodic reverse-filtration; reduces IDH incidence, improves β₂-microglobulin removal, minimizes albumin loss
Clinical: Hemodynamic stability + enhanced middle-molecule clearance
Numbers: 17% Japanese dialysis patients use I-HDF (>55,000 patients by 2022)

## Cognitive Deterioration in HD Patients (2025)
DOI: 10.3389/fnagi.2025.1645702
Design: Narrative review
Key: Age-stratified mechanisms: children—BBB permeability + neuroplasticity; elderly—vascular disease + AD pathology; universal benefit from incremental/low-temp/high-dose HDF
Clinical: Personalized interventions reduce inflammation/oxidative stress across ages
Numbers: N/A

## Sepsis in HD Patients (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/life15091488
Design: Narrative review (37 studies)
Key: Sepsis multifactorial in HD; diabetes/age/CVC strong risk; non-vascular sources substantial; delayed diagnosis/suboptimal empiric therapy key drivers
Clinical: Early recognition + individualized ABx strategy essential
Numbers: Sepsis incidence >12/100 patient-years; mortality remains high

## BP Management on Dialysis: UK Guidelines (2025)
DOI: 10.1186/s12882-025-04451-2
Design: Clinical practice guideline (systematic review–based)
Key: Comprehensive BP management: accurate measurement, volume control, lifestyle, dialysate sodium, targeted antihypertensives; mitigates IDH risk
Clinical: Reduces CV events and death in 90% hypertensive HD population
Numbers: 86% HD, 65–92% PD hypertensive

## Fish Oil and CV Events in HD (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2513032
Design: Double-blind RCT, 1,228 patients (610 fish oil vs 618 placebo), 3.5 years
Key: 4g n-3 PUFA daily (1.6g EPA + 0.8g DHA) cuts major CV events 43% (HR 0.57, P<0.001); cardiac death HR 0.55, stroke HR 0.37
Clinical: Novel cardioprotection in dialysis; first positive CV outcome trial in HD
Numbers: 0.31 vs 0.61 events/1000 patient-days; HR 0.77 (95% CI 0.65–0.90) including non-CV death

## Porcine Kidney Xenotransplantation (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2412747
Design: Case report
Key: First human xenotransplant—62yo with 69-edit porcine kidney; immediate function, Cr drop, dialysis-independent; T-cell rejection reversed; cardiac death day 52
Clinical: Proof-of-concept; expands access; requires refined patient selection
Numbers: 52-day survival; no graft rejection at autopsy

Clinical: Not relevant to nephrology/dialysis
Numbers: N/A

## CKD-Associated Pruritus in HD (2025)
DOI: 10.1093/ckj/sfaf096
Design: Narrative review + diagnostic algorithm
Key: CKD-aP affects 31–40% (53.5% any severity); bilateral symmetric non-dermatomal distribution; requires differential diagnosis from dermatologic/systemic/neuropathic causes
Clinical: Improves QoL; systematic workup and tiered treatment strategy
Numbers: 31.2% moderate-to-severe; significant burden underrecognized

## Water Utilization in HD (2025)
DOI: 10.1159/000546034
Design: Narrative review
Key: HD consumes 408–490L/session; RO waste 70% (~350L/session); proposes waste reuse (irrigation, toilet) and technology innovation (NxStage PureFlow, Aquaboss)
Clinical: Environmental stewardship; cost reduction
Numbers: 54% total water wasted; newer RO systems reduce waste to 20%

## HDF as Primary Modality: PRO (2025)
DOI: 10.34067/KID.0000000614
Design: Position paper
Key: High-volume HDF (>23L/week convection) reduces all-cause mortality 23% (CONVINCE), 16% (IPD-MA); superior QoL; FDA clearance 2024
Clinical: HD outcomes suboptimal; HDF now technically available, cost-effective
Numbers: CONVINCE HR 0.77 all-cause death; IPD-MA HR 0.84 all-cause, HR 0.67 cardiac death

## HDF as Primary Modality: Commentary (2025)
DOI: 10.34067/KID.0000000847
Design: Commentary
Key: Acknowledges HDF benefits but urges caution: extend high-flux HD to 4h first; uncertain US cost model; financial sustainability unproven
Clinical: Balanced approach; pilot US data needed before large-scale adoption
Numbers: N/A

## HDF as Primary Modality: CON (2025)
DOI: 10.34067/KID.0000000783
Design: Position paper
Key: HDF trials exclusively European (healthier, younger, >80% white); US patients older, higher comorbidity (CAD 2.2×, DM 1.7× higher); needs US safety/efficacy RCT
Clinical: Evidence not generalizable to US population; extrapolation risky
Numbers: Ethnic disparity—CONTRAST 85% white vs US 46%; age gap 3 years

## Strategies to Prevent CVC Dysfunction (2025)
DOI: 10.1681/ASN.0000000666
Design: Narrative review + clinical guidance
Key: CVC dysfunction affects ~20% patients; optimize lock solutions, catheter design, QI bundles; paradigm shifted to patient-centered access selection
Clinical: Reduces mechanical/infectious complications; essential for vulnerable populations
Numbers: CVC prevalence 60–85% incident HD (US 85%, Japan 2%); 25–45% prevalent HD
