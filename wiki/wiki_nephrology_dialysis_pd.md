# Nephrology Dialysis PD — Wiki

## Overcoming Barriers for Uptake and Continued Use of Home Dialysis (2020)
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2019.11.007
Design: Conference Report (NKF-KDOQI)
Key: Recommendations to increase home dialysis (PD, HDHD) via hub-and-spoke model, peer mentorship, care partner support, telehealth, and Medicare reimbursement reform.
Clinical: Identifies barriers to home dialysis adoption in US; proposes education, program structure, and policy changes; CMS target: 80% new ESRD patients on home dialysis or pre-emptive transplant by 2025.
Numbers: N/A

## KRT in Remote Extreme Area: The Case of Easter Island (2025)
DOI: 10.34067/KID.0000000833
Design: Perspective
Key: Easter Island (population 8445) established hybrid HD/telemedicine model for ESKD after failed PD attempts; now provides 6-day/week HD with remote nephrologist supervision.
Clinical: Demonstrates feasibility of dialysis in remote resource-limited settings; prioritized HD after poor PD outcomes (low adherence, peritonitis, contamination); combines on-site trained nurses/technician with 24/7 remote nephrologist and 5-6 week in-person visits.
Numbers: Island population 8445; HD 6 days/week in two shifts; remote physician oversight

## How I Treat a Hospitalized Patient Receiving Peritoneal Dialysis (2025)
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.0000000783
Design: Narrative Review (How I Treat series)
Key: Hospital PD management complicated by shortage of trained staff; rising inpatient PD volumes demand standardized protocols; case review of 64yo diabetic on APD with gangrene requiring inpatient management.
Clinical: Highlights underappreciated gap in hospital PD guidelines; non-peritonitis infections now exceed peritonitis as cause of PD patient hospitalization.
Numbers: US PD prevalence 8.3% of ESKD; PD inpatient rate 1.47/person-year (2020–2021); hospitalization rising as home dialysis programs expand

## Association of Stratified Benchmarks With Financial Penalties in the ESRD Treatment Choices Model (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.5209
Design: Observational cohort analysis (CMS ETC model data)
Key: Stratified benchmarks (DE/LIS ≥50% vs <50%) in CMS ESRD Treatment Choices model reduced disproportionate penalties on safety-net dialysis facilities; equity gap narrowed.
Clinical: Policy analysis showing CMS stratification improved fairness in performance-based reimbursement; facilities serving high-poverty populations no longer systematically penalized after 2022 implementation.
Numbers: 2259 facilities; penalties 13.8% (2021) → 20.7% (2022) overall; DE/LIS ≥50% facilities 22.6% (2021) → 19.5% (2022); high-poverty facilities penalty decrease reversed post-stratification

## ISPD GUIDELINES & POSITION PAPERS

### ISPD Peritonitis Guideline 2022 Update — Li2022ISPDPeritonitis
DOI: 10.1177/08968608221080586
Design: Clinical practice guideline (ISPD)
Key: Comprehensive peritonitis prevention + treatment. New targets: overall rate ≤0.40 ep/yr, culture-negative <15%, peritonitis-free >80%/yr. Empiric Abx: cefazolin + gentamicin (or ceftazidime) IP. Duration: GP 14d, GN 14-21d, Pseudomonas 21d. Catheter removal: refractory (day 5), fungal, mycobacterial. Return to PD ≥2wk after removal.
Clinical: Replaces 2016 version. Adds proton pump inhibitor caution (H2RA preferred for gastroprotection on PD). Low-K hypokalemia → peritonitis risk. Pet ownership → exit-site hygiene counseling.

### ISPD Catheter-Related Infection 2023 — Chow2023CatheterInfection
DOI: 10.1177/08968608231172740
Design: Clinical practice guideline (ISPD)
Key: Revised exit-site infection (ESI) + tunnel infection definitions. New ESI rate target ≤0.40 ep/yr. Topical Abx prophylaxis downgraded (mupirocin/gentamicin cream). Abx course can be shortened to 7-10d based on clinical response. Surgical salvage: outer cuff shaving, exit-site relocation.
Clinical: Exit-site scoring system endorsed (Twardowski). Prophylactic Abx before dental/GI procedures NOT routinely recommended.

### ISPD PD Access 2019 — Crabtree2019PDAccess
DOI: 10.3747/pdi.2018.00232
Design: Clinical practice guideline (ISPD)
Key: Catheter: silicone, double-cuff Dacron preferred. Four insertion techniques (percutaneous, open surgical, peritoneoscopic, laparoscopic). Advanced laparoscopic: rectus sheath tunneling + omentopexy reduce complications. Break-in ≥2wk ideal. Special situations: PKD, bariatric surgery, aortic stent.
Clinical: Quality audit benchmarks: primary catheter failure <8%, 1yr catheter survival >80%.

### ISPD Goal-Directed PD 2020 — Brown2020GoalDirectedPD
DOI: 10.1177/0896860819895364
Design: Practice recommendations (ISPD)
Key: Paradigm shift from "adequacy" (Kt/V target) to "goal-directed" PD. Integrate patient goals, volume status, nutrition, RRF, small-solute clearance. SDM central. Incremental PD endorsed. Holistic assessment > single biomarker.
Clinical: Applies to adults, children, LMIC. Replaced prescriptive Kt/V-centric approach. Framework: symptom burden + QoL + volume + clearance + nutrition.

### ISPD Solute/Fluid Removal Targets 2019 Update — Boudville2019SoluteFluid
DOI: 10.1177/0896860819898307
Design: Guideline update (ISPD, updates 2005 original)
Key: RRF consistently associated with mortality (preserve aggressively). Peritoneal clearance–mortality link inconsistent. Kt/V >1.7-1.8 no survival benefit (GRADE 1B). Anuric patients: minimum Kt/V 1.7. UF volume associated with survival but no numeric target possible. Icodextrin recommended to improve UF.
Clinical: Key practical message — RRF preservation > pushing peritoneal clearance. Stop ACEi/ARB debate ongoing; avoid nephrotoxins.

### ISPD EPS Position Paper 2017 Update — Brown2017EPS
DOI: 10.3747/pdi.2017.00018
Design: Position paper (ISPD)
Key: EPS = rare but severe long-term PD complication (mainly PD >5yr). No reliable predictive biomarkers. Do NOT limit PD duration based on EPS fear alone. SDM: individualized assessment (age, PD vintage, dialysis quality, transplant access, HD risk, QoL).
Clinical: Incidence 0.5-4.4% in various registries. CT scan not validated for screening. Fast PSTR may signal membrane changes but poor EPS predictor.

### ISPD Membrane Dysfunction 2021 — Morelle2021MembraneDysfunction
DOI: 10.1177/0896860820982218
Design: Practice guideline (ISPD)
Key: Pathophysiology taxonomy: (1) fast PSTR (high transport → UF failure from glucose absorption), (2) intrinsic low UF (aquaporin-1 dysfunction), (3) acquired membrane injury (sclerosing peritonitis). 4h PET measures PSTR (GRADE 1A). UF volume as screening tool (GRADE 1B). Sodium dip diagnoses low osmotic conductance to glucose (GRADE 2B).
Clinical: Practical flowchart: UF failure → repeat PET → classify mechanism → adjust prescription (APD short dwells for fast PSTR, icodextrin for type 1+2) → or transfer to HD.

### ISPD Teaching PD 2025 — Chow2025TeachingPD
DOI: 10.1177/08968608251375512
Design: Position paper (ISPD)
Key: PD nurse trainer qualifications, pre-training assessment (literacy, dexterity, home environment), training methods (recommended ≥5 sessions over ≥5 days, 1:1 preferred, competency-based). Home visits pre- and post-training. Virtual training acceptable as supplement. PROMs endorsed for outcome measurement.
Clinical: Evidence quality low (mostly expert consensus). Key gap: no validated competency assessment tool. Training duration ≥6d associated with lower peritonitis (PDOPPS data).

### ISPD Assisted PD 2024 — Oliver2024AssistedPD
DOI: 10.1177/08968608241246447
Design: Position paper (ISPD, first on this topic)
Key: Assisted PD = health-system-funded professional/trained personnel providing PD assistance at patient's home. Seven themes: candidacy assessment, care models (nurse-assisted CAPD/APD, family-assisted, iAPD), training/communication, quality control, funding models, impact on PD utilization, research gaps.
Clinical: France 51% assisted PD (highest globally). Enables elderly/frail patients. Reduces nursing home HD dependence. Cost-effective vs in-center HD. Key barrier: funding model varies by country.

## CENTER EFFECTS & VOLUME-OUTCOME

### Centre Characteristics and PD Peritonitis Risk (RDPLF) — Bechade2017Nurses
DOI: 10.1093/ndt/gfx051
Design: Retrospective registry cohort (RDPLF, France, 5017 patients, 127 centers, 2008-2012)
Key: Dedicated PD nurse cs-HR 0.75 (0.67-0.83) = 25% peritonitis risk reduction (strongest modifiable center factor). Pre-dialysis home visit cs-HR 0.87 (0.76-0.97) = 13% reduction. Post-dialysis home visit NS. Center type (university/community/private) NS. Center volume NS. Center random-effect variance 0.11; adjusting center characteristics reduced variance 35%.
Clinical: Actionable — hire PD-dedicated nurses + pre-dialysis home visits. Size/type don't matter; processes do. Complements Htay2018 (ANZDATA).

### ANZDATA: Center Characteristics and PD Technique Failure — Htay2017ANZDATA
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.12321216
Design: Registry cohort (ANZDATA, Australia, 9362 incident PD patients, 51 centers, 2004-2014)
Key: 7-fold variation in technique failure rates across centers. Independent risk factors for technique failure: small center (<16 incident/yr), low PD proportion (<18% of dialysis), low phosphate target attainment (<40%). Center factors explained 53% of variance after patient adjustment. Center volume threshold ~16/yr.
Clinical: PD proportion (commitment) matters more than absolute volume. Reinforces center-level quality metrics.

### ANZDATA: Center Effects on PD Peritonitis Outcomes — Htay2018ANZDATAPeritonitis
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2017.10.017
Design: Retrospective registry cohort (ANZDATA, 51 centers, 4428 patients, 9100 peritonitis episodes)
Key: Center effect on peritonitis cure rate >> patient effect. Adjusting center characteristics reduced cross-center variance 66%; patient characteristics only 9%. Higher PD proportion + complete empiric Abx coverage independently associated with better outcomes.
Clinical: Complements Bechade2017 — ANZDATA has organism data (Bechade lacks it), Bechade has nursing data (ANZDATA lacks it). Combined picture: PD commitment + dedicated nurses + guideline-adherent Abx.

### RDPLF Volume-Outcome (Monte Carlo) — Evans2013RDPLF
DOI: 10.1136/bmjopen-2013-003092
Design: Registry cohort + Monte Carlo simulation (RDPLF, 9602 PD patients, France)
Key: High-volume centers: technique failure cs-HR 0.46 (significant). No effect on mortality or transplant. Robust to unmeasured confounders (E-value analysis). Monte Carlo policy simulation: merging small into large centers → modest net improvement only.
Clinical: Volume-outcome exists for technique failure but policy effect of centralization is limited. Supports "improve small centers" over "close small centers."

### Systematic Review: Dialysis Volume-Outcome — Pieper2015VolumeOutcome
DOI: 10.1186/s13104-015-1785-5
Design: Systematic review (MEDLINE + EMBASE, to Nov 2014)
Key: 12 studies found. HD: inconsistent volume-outcome relationship. PD: consistent — higher center volume associated with better technique survival. No evidence for regionalization benefit. Practice-makes-perfect vs selective-referral not distinguishable.
Clinical: PD has stronger volume-outcome signal than HD. Threshold varies (20-50 patients). No RCTs.

### PDOPPS: PD Infection Rates and Outcomes — Perl2020PDOPPS
DOI: 10.1053/j.ajkd.2019.09.016
Design: Prospective cohort (PDOPPS, 7 countries, 209 centers, 7051 patients)
Key: Overall peritonitis rate 0.28 ep/patient-yr. Country range 0.26-0.40. Higher APD proportion, perioperative Abx prophylaxis, ≥6-day training → lower peritonitis risk. Gram-positive dominant (40-50%). Center-level variation large.
Clinical: Largest international PD infection comparison. Training ≥6 days = modifiable factor. Benchmarks by country useful.

## GLOBAL PD SYSTEMS

### Global Dialysis Perspective: Canada — Blake2022Canada
DOI: 10.34067/KID.0000462019
Design: Country review (Kidney360)
Key: Single-payer universal system. Hub-and-spoke model: 27 Renal Programs in Ontario → ~100 HD sites. PD 20%, HHD 5% (total home 25%). Modality-independent physician capitation fee in ON/AB/QC removes in-center HD incentive. For-profit dialysis essentially absent. Funding: Ontario modality-dependent bundles (Center HD CA$51K/yr vs Home HD CA$23K/yr = 45% cost).
Clinical: Exemplar of system-level PD promotion via funding alignment. Capitation removes physician-level modality bias. Satellite HD = hub-spoke implemented nationally.

### Global Dialysis Perspective: Australia — Polkinghorne2022Australia
DOI: 10.34067/KID.0000112019
Design: Country review (Kidney360)
Key: ANZDATA registry covers all KRT. PD ~18% prevalent. Indigenous Australians ESKD incidence 3× general population. Public universal healthcare. Government funds dialysis through states/territories. HDF gaining share from conventional HD. Satellite HD expanding. Home HD ~10%. CKD prevalence 10%. ESKD incidence ~120 pmppa.
Clinical: Strong registry culture. Indigenous health = major PD challenge (remote, hot climate, crowded housing). ANZDATA = gold standard for center-level benchmarking.

## COMMUNITY & REMOTE PD

### Remote PD Outcomes: Guangzhou — Yi2016Guangzhou
DOI: 10.1159/000442516
Design: Retrospective cohort (single center, 1005 remote PD patients, South China)
Key: Comprehensive follow-up program (clinic visits, phone support, 24h hotline, satellite centers, home visits). 1/3/5-yr patient survival 95.4/84.7/71.8%. 1/3/5-yr technique survival 98.6/92.3/83.4%. Peritonitis rate 0.16 ep/patient-yr (excellent). Independent mortality risk: older age, DM, peritonitis-free survival short, irregular clinic visits, low Hb.
Clinical: Demonstrates remote PD feasible with structured follow-up. Hub (大學醫院 Guangzhou) + satellite centers + telemedicine = de facto hub-spoke in developing country.

### RM-APD vs Conventional APD in Taiwan — Chung2025Taiwan
→ See dedicated wiki: [Chung2025Taiwan.md](/proj/wiki/Chung2025Taiwan.md)
DOI: 10.1111/nep.70061. Taiwan TCVGH n=177. RM-APD peritonitis HR 0.39 (0.15-0.96). Zero relapse, zero infection-driven HD transfer.

### CQI Impact on PD Outcomes: Jinling — Yu2014CQI
DOI: 10.3747/pdi.2013.00123
Design: Before-after cohort (single center, Nanjing Jinling Hospital, 2005-2011)
Key: CQI program: dedicated PD team (2008), standardized protocols, monthly KPI tracking. PD census 50→510. Peritonitis rate dropped. CQI elements: connect-disconnect protocol, exit-site care, mandatory retraining, nurse-patient ratio. Province-level equal PD/HD reimbursement (2010) enabled growth.
Clinical: CQI = scalable model for expanding PD in developing settings. Equal reimbursement policy + CQI team = necessary combination. Relevant to Taiwan community PD proposal.

## PD EPIDEMIOLOGY & TEXTBOOK

### Current Status and Growth of PD (Nolph & Gokal Ch.2) — NG2023_Ch02
DOI: 10.1007/978-3-030-62087-5_41
Design: Textbook chapter (Nolph & Gokal 4th ed, 2023)
Key: Global PD utilization 2-79% (Hong Kong highest). Growth in developing countries (cost advantage, less infrastructure). Barriers: physician bias, lack of training, funding inequality, vascular access availability paradox. PD survival comparable to HD in first 2-3 years (advantage in younger/non-DM). RRF preserved longer on PD. Assisted PD expanding access to elderly.
Clinical: Comprehensive reference for PD epidemiology + barriers + outcomes.
