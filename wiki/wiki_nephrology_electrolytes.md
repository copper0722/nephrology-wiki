# Nephrology Electrolytes — Journal Reading Wiki

## Sodium Zirconium Cyclosilicate in Hyperkalemia (2015)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa1411487
Design: RCT, phase 3, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter
Key: ZS-9 (oral non-absorbable cation exchanger) reduces serum K+ by exponential decline at 48h initial phase; maintains normalization through 14-day maintenance phase.
Clinical: Manages hyperkalemia without dialysis; allows RAAS inhibitor continuation in CKD, HF, diabetic patients.
Numbers: Initial K+ range 5.0–6.5 mmol/L; target normalization 3.5–4.9 mmol/L; doses 1.25–10g TDS.

## Patiromer in Kidney Disease and Hyperkalemia (OPAL-HK trial, 2015)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa1410853
Design: RCT, phase 3, single-blind, multicenter, two-stage
Key: Non-absorbable polymer patiromer binds K+ in distal colon; effective & safe in CKD stage 3–4 on RAAS inhibitors, eliminates gut toxicity of older polystyrene resins.
Clinical: Enables prolonged RAAS inhibitor therapy in hyperkalemic CKD; improves tolerability for outpatient management.
Numbers: eGFR 15–<60 ml/min/1.73m²; baseline K+ 5.1–<6.5 mmol/L; age 18–80.
