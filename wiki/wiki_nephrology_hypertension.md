# Nephrology Hypertension — Journal Reading Wiki

## 2014 JNC 8 Evidence-Based Hypertension Guideline (2014)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2013.284427
Design: Guideline
Key: BP targets <150/90 for age ≥60 y, <140/90 for 30-59 y; ACEi/ARB/CCB/thiazide for non-Black; CCB/thiazide for Black; ACEi/ARB preferred in CKD.
Clinical: Practical treatment thresholds and drug selection by population and comorbidity.
Numbers: Age-stratified targets; 4 first-line drug classes for general population; CKD = same target as age <60.

## Clinical Pharmacology of Antihypertensive Therapy in CKD (2019)
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.04330418
Design: Narrative review
Key: Comprehensive pharmacology of ACEi, ARB, β-blockers, CCB, thiazides, loop diuretics, MRAs, vasodilators, α-agonists; β-blockers and thiazides may have expanded role in late CKD.
Clinical: Evidence-based drug selection for CKD and ESKD; resistant HTN common in this cohort.
Numbers: 60% of CKD cohort on ≥3 antihypertensive agents.

## Insights into Salt Handling and Blood Pressure (2021)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra2030212
Design: Review article
Key: Salt restriction reduces SBP 0.4 mmHg (normal BP) to 4 mmHg (hypertensive); K+/Na+ ratio critical; K-enriched salt (75% NaCl/25% KCl) reduces stroke, major CV events, all-cause mortality.
Clinical: Individual salt sensitivity varies widely; practical clinical definition still lacking; K/Na balance more important than absolute Na reduction.
Numbers: SBP reduction 0.4–4 mmHg depending on baseline; K-salt composition 75/25.

## Primary Aldosteronism: Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guideline (2025)
DOI: 10.1210/clinem/dgaf284
Design: Guideline
Key: Screen all hypertensive patients with aldosterone/renin ratio; PA-specific treatment (drug or surgery); MRAs preferred over ENaC inhibitors; spironolactone recommended first-line for cost/availability.
Clinical: Addresses PA underdiagnosis and undertreatment; standardizes screening and management pathways.
Numbers: N/A

## Cardiovascular-Kidney-Metabolic Effects: Steroidal and Nonsteroidal MRAs (2025)
DOI: 10.31083/RCM38690
Design: Narrative review
Key: Steroidal MRAs effective in HFrEF and resistant HTN (reduced CV death, hospitalization, SBP); nonsteroidal MRA finerenone effective in HFmrEF/HFpEF and T2D-CKD (reduced CV events and CKD progression).
Clinical: MRAs address CV-kidney-metabolic syndrome; expanding evidence for nonsteroidal agents in expanding HF and CKD phenotypes.
Numbers: ~620 million CV disease patients globally; ~57 million HF cases (2021).

## 2025 AHA/ACC/AANP Adult Hypertension Prevention, Detection, Assessment, Management Guideline (2025)
DOI: 10.1161/CIR.0000000000001356
Design: Guideline
Key: Updated comprehensive BP management guideline with modernized methodology; includes PREVENT equations and SPRINT trial evidence.
Clinical: Current multiorganizational standard for adult HTN prevention and management.
Numbers: N/A

## Lorundrostat Efficacy and Safety in Uncontrolled Hypertension (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2501440
Design: RCT (Advance-HTN Phase 2b)
Key: Lorundrostat (aldosterone synthase inhibitor) added to standardized background therapy (olmesartan + thiazide ± amlodipine) in resistant HTN; 103 US sites; strict renal and electrolyte entry criteria (eGFR ≥45, K+ ≤5.0, Na+ ≥135).
Clinical: Novel aldosterone synthase inhibitor for treatment-resistant hypertension; electrolyte management essential.
Numbers: 103 sites; inclusion SBP 140–180, DBP 65–110 (or DBP 90–110 regardless of SBP); eGFR ≥45 ml/min/1.73m².
