# Kidney Disease in Pregnancy — Board Review

## Exam Logic Summary

Pregnancy-related nephrology tests: (1) physiologic renal changes (↑ GFR, ↓ creatinine, physiologic hydronephrosis), (2) differentiating preeclampsia from other TMA causes, (3) AKI etiologies unique to pregnancy by trimester, (4) drug safety in pregnancy, (5) CKD impact on pregnancy outcomes. Common distractor: treating physiologic hydronephrosis of pregnancy as obstruction.

## Physiologic Renal Changes in Pregnancy

### Hemodynamic Changes

| Parameter | Change | Mechanism | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| GFR | ↑ 40-65% | ↑ renal plasma flow, ↓ afferent arteriolar resistance | Peaks 1st trimester, sustained |
| Serum creatinine | ↓ to 0.4-0.6 mg/dL | ↑ GFR | Normal sCr 0.8 mg/dL in pregnancy = concerning |
| BUN | ↓ to 8-10 mg/dL | ↑ clearance | - |
| Uric acid | ↓ early, ↑ late | ↑ clearance early; ↓ excretion late | ↑ uric acid in late pregnancy may signal preeclampsia |
| Renal plasma flow | ↑ 50-80% | ↑ cardiac output, ↓ SVR, vasodilation (relaxin, NO, progesterone) | Peaks 2nd trimester |
| Blood pressure | ↓ 10 mmHg by mid-pregnancy | Systemic vasodilation | Nadir at 20 weeks; returns to baseline by term |
| Plasma volume | ↑ 40-50% | Aldosterone-mediated Na retention | Progressive; dilutional anemia |
| Serum Na+ | ↓ 4-5 mEq/L (135 normal in pregnancy) | Reset osmostat (↓ threshold for ADH and thirst) | - |

### Anatomic Changes

- **Physiologic hydronephrosis:** Right > left (due to dextrorotated uterus compressing right ureter + right ovarian vein crossing ureter). Present in up to 80% of pregnancies. NOT pathologic.
- Kidney size increases ~1 cm
- Ureteral dilation (progesterone-mediated smooth muscle relaxation)

### Proteinuria in Pregnancy

- Normal: up to 300 mg/24h (vs 150 mg in non-pregnant)
- Spot UPCR < 300 mg/g considered normal
- Preeclampsia threshold: >= 300 mg/24h OR UPCR >= 300 mg/g

**Exam pearl:** sCr >= 0.8 mg/dL in pregnancy should raise concern for kidney dysfunction. A "normal" sCr of 1.0 mg/dL in pregnancy represents significant GFR reduction.

## AKI During Pregnancy

### Epidemiology

- Pregnancy-related AKI: 1-2.8% of pregnancies in developed countries (higher in developing)
- Most common in 3rd trimester and peripartum period
- Maternal mortality 5-20% (higher in developing countries)

### Etiologies by Trimester

| Trimester | Causes |
|---|---|
| **1st** | Hyperemesis gravidarum (prerenal), septic abortion, ectopic pregnancy hemorrhage |
| **2nd** | Placental abruption, HELLP syndrome (rare this early), UTI/pyelonephritis |
| **3rd/Peripartum** | **Preeclampsia/eclampsia** (most common), HELLP, TTP/aHUS, acute fatty liver of pregnancy (AFLP), hemorrhage (placenta previa, abruption, postpartum), amniotic fluid embolism, postpartum AKI |

### Preeclampsia vs Other TMA Causes (HIGH-YIELD COMPARISON)

| Feature | Preeclampsia/HELLP | TTP | aHUS | AFLP |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Timing | > 20 weeks (usually 3rd trimester) | Any trimester | Postpartum (usually) | 3rd trimester |
| HTN | Yes (defining) | Variable | Variable | Variable |
| Proteinuria | Yes (defining) | Uncommon | Common | Variable |
| Platelets | Moderately ↓ | Severely ↓ (< 30K) | Moderately ↓ | Moderately ↓ |
| LDH/schistocytes | ↑ in HELLP | Markedly ↑ | ↑ | Variable |
| ADAMTS13 | Normal | < 10% (diagnostic) | Normal | Normal |
| Liver involvement | ↑ AST/ALT (HELLP) | Mild | Mild | Severe (↑↑ bilirubin, hypoglycemia, ↑ ammonia, coagulopathy) |
| Treatment | Delivery | Plasma exchange | Eculizumab | Delivery |
| Postpartum resolution | Yes (within days-weeks) | Responds to PEX | Does NOT resolve with delivery | Yes with delivery |

**Exam pearl:** Postpartum AKI that does NOT resolve after delivery → consider aHUS (complement-mediated). ADAMTS13 normal distinguishes from TTP. Eculizumab is treatment.

### Preeclampsia — Renal Pathology

- Glomerular endotheliosis (swollen endothelial cells, loss of capillary lumen) — pathognomonic on biopsy
- sFlt-1/PlGF ratio: sFlt-1 (soluble fms-like tyrosine kinase 1) ↑, PlGF (placental growth factor) ↓
- Angiogenic imbalance → endothelial dysfunction → HTN + proteinuria + organ damage

### Cortical Necrosis

- Rare but devastating cause of pregnancy-related AKI (more common in developing countries)
- Associated with placental abruption, septic abortion, severe DIC
- Bilateral cortical necrosis → irreversible AKI → ESKD
- CT: non-enhancing cortex with preserved medulla

### Management of AKI in Pregnancy

- **Volume resuscitation** for prerenal causes
- **Delivery** for preeclampsia/HELLP/AFLP when >= 34 weeks (or earlier if severe)
- **Magnesium sulfate** for seizure prophylaxis in severe preeclampsia/eclampsia
- **Antihypertensive:** labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa (SAFE). **Contraindicated:** ACEi, ARB (teratogenic — renal agenesis, oligohydramnios, calvarial hypoplasia)
- **RRT** if indicated (CRRT preferred hemodynamically)
- **Plasma exchange** for TTP
- **Eculizumab** for aHUS

## Pregnancy in Patients with Underlying CKD

### Risk Stratification

| CKD Stage | eGFR | Pregnancy risk |
|---|---|---|
| 1-2 | >= 60 | Low-moderate risk; good outcomes if BP controlled and proteinuria minimal |
| 3 | 30-59 | Moderate-high risk; ↑ preeclampsia, preterm delivery, FGR |
| 4-5 | < 30 | High risk; ↑↑ maternal and fetal complications; counseling essential |
| 5D (dialysis) | - | Highest risk; fertility reduced but pregnancy possible; intensive dialysis improves outcomes |

### Outcomes in CKD Pregnancies

- **Accelerated CKD progression:** pregnancy may accelerate decline, especially if sCr > 1.4 mg/dL at conception
- **Preeclampsia risk:** 2-5x higher than general population; difficult to distinguish superimposed preeclampsia from CKD progression
- **Preterm delivery:** 30-60% in CKD stage 3-5
- **Fetal growth restriction:** correlates with severity of maternal kidney disease and proteinuria
- **Maternal outcomes:** ↑ risk of permanent GFR decline, especially if baseline sCr > 1.5-2.0 mg/dL

### Dialysis and Pregnancy

- Intensive HD (>= 36 hours/week or nocturnal HD) improves outcomes: live birth rate ~80% vs ~50% with conventional HD
- Target predialysis BUN < 50 mg/dL (some advocate < 36)
- Avoid hypotension during dialysis (fetal perfusion dependent on maternal BP)
- Minimize heparin or switch to citrate (bleeding risk)
- Adjust dry weight frequently (weight gain expected in pregnancy)
- EPO requirements increase (pregnancy + dialysis)
- PD: may need to reduce fill volumes as uterus enlarges; transition to HD in 3rd trimester

### Transplant and Pregnancy

- Wait >= 1 year post-transplant (some guidelines: >= 2 years) before conception
- Stable graft function (sCr < 1.5 mg/dL), minimal proteinuria, controlled BP
- Immunosuppression adjustments: continue tacrolimus, azathioprine, prednisone. **Discontinue:** MMF (teratogenic — category X), sirolimus, everolimus
- Monitor tacrolimus levels closely (volume of distribution changes)
- Preeclampsia risk remains elevated (3-4x)
- Graft loss risk not significantly increased if stable function at conception

### Drug Safety in Pregnancy (HIGH-YIELD TABLE)

| Drug | Safety | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ACEi/ARB | **CONTRAINDICATED** | Teratogenic: renal agenesis, oligohydramnios, calvarial hypoplasia, neonatal renal failure |
| Labetalol | Safe | First-line antihypertensive |
| Nifedipine | Safe | Second-line antihypertensive |
| Methyldopa | Safe | Alternative; may cause depression |
| Hydralazine | Safe (IV) | For hypertensive emergencies |
| MgSO4 | Safe | Seizure prophylaxis; monitor for Mg toxicity (loss of reflexes) |
| Azathioprine | Safe | Immunosuppression; fetal liver lacks enzyme to convert to active metabolite |
| Tacrolimus | Safe (monitor levels) | Dose adjustment needed |
| Cyclosporine | Relatively safe | Monitor BP and renal function |
| MMF | **CONTRAINDICATED** | Category X — teratogenic |
| Cyclophosphamide | **CONTRAINDICATED** | Teratogenic |
| Rituximab | Avoid (limited data) | B-cell depletion in neonate |
| ESAs (EPO, darbepoetin) | Safe | May need dose increase |
| IV iron | Safe (2nd/3rd trimester) | Avoid 1st trimester (limited data) |

## High-Yield Exam Scenarios

1. **28-week pregnant woman with BP 160/100, proteinuria 2 g/24h, sCr 1.2, platelets 85K, ↑ LDH:** HELLP syndrome. Deliver (>= 34 weeks or if severe at any gestational age). MgSO4 for seizure prophylaxis. Labetalol for BP.

2. **Postpartum day 5, AKI not improving despite delivery, platelets 60K, LDH elevated, sCr rising:** Consider aHUS (complement-mediated). Check ADAMTS13 — if normal → not TTP → start eculizumab. aHUS does NOT resolve with delivery alone.

3. **CKD patient wanting to conceive, on losartan + MMF:** Must switch BEFORE conception. Losartan → labetalol or nifedipine. MMF → azathioprine. Wait until stable on new regimen.

4. **Pregnant woman with sCr 0.9 mg/dL — is this normal?** Borderline concerning. Normal pregnancy sCr = 0.4-0.6 mg/dL due to ↑ GFR. sCr 0.9 = ~50% higher than expected → evaluate for kidney disease.

5. **Pregnant patient with right flank pain and US showing right hydronephrosis:** Likely physiologic (right > left, present in 80%). No intervention needed unless fever/infection or bilateral/left-sided (more concerning).
