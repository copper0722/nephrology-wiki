# Nutrition Metabolism — Part1 — Journal Reading Wiki

## Effect of Hemodialysis Prescription on Morbidity (1981)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa198111123052002
Design: RCT
Key: Morbidity correlated with time-averaged blood urea nitrogen (TAC_urea); higher BUN associated with increased hospitalizations and study withdrawals despite equal mortality across groups.
Clinical: Establishes quantitative dialysis adequacy targets; higher BUN worsens outcomes even without mortality difference.
Numbers: N=151, 4 groups (high/low BUN × short/long treatment time); hospitalizations and withdrawals higher in high-BUN groups.

## Mechanistic Analysis of NCDS (1985)
DOI: 10.1038/ki.1985.166
Design: Mechanistic analysis
Key: Kt/V as discontinuous function of probability of clinical failure; PCR-dependent modeling shows nutritional adequacy critical when Kt/V adequate.
Clinical: Introduces Kt/V as dialysis adequacy metric; highlights protein-energy balance interdependence in uremia.
Numbers: N/A

## Evolution of the Kidney (2016)
DOI: 10.19080/APBIJ.2016.01.555554
Design: Review
Key: Vertebrate kidneys evolve through pronephros→mesonephros→metanephros sequence; kidney structure and function adapt to osmotic challenges in different environments.
Clinical: Comparative anatomy basis for understanding nephron physiology and evolutionary constraints on renal function.
Numbers: N/A

## Effects of Intermittent Fasting on Health, Aging, and Disease (2019)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra1905136
Design: Narrative review
Key: Intermittent fasting triggers metabolic switching to ketone utilization and adaptive cellular stress responses independent of weight loss; improves insulin sensitivity, stress resistance, and inflammation markers.
Clinical: Potential disease-modifying approach for obesity, diabetes, CVD, neurodegeneration; mechanisms beyond caloric deficit.
Numbers: N/A

## Tirzepatide Once Weekly for the Treatment of Obesity (2022)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2206038
Design: RCT, Phase 3 (SURMOUNT-1)
Key: GIP/GLP-1 dual agonist dose-dependently reduces body weight with 15mg achieving ~22% weight reduction; well-tolerated with GI side effects manageable.
Clinical: Novel dual-incretin therapy option for obesity; synergistic GIP+GLP-1 mechanisms superior to monotherapy.
Numbers: N=2,539; tirzepatide 15mg: -21.9% vs placebo -2.6% (p<0.001); ≥5% weight loss: 85% vs 35%.

## Diets (2024)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra2211889
Design: Narrative review
Key: Plant-predominant diets (Mediterranean, DASH, plant-based variants) most consistently linked to reduced chronic disease risk via reduced inflammation, improved lipids, enhanced insulin sensitivity.
Clinical: Dietary patterns preferable to single nutrients; Mediterranean diet best-studied with robust evidence across multiple outcomes.
Numbers: N/A

## Dietary Phosphorus and Metabolic Health in CKD and ESKD (2025)
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.0000000715
Design: Narrative review
Key: Phosphate restriction in CKD reduces cardiovascular risk via lowering FGF23, vascular calcification; organic plant phosphate less bioavailable than processed food additives.
Clinical: Phosphate management parallel to protein restriction; additive phosphate burden from Western diet processed foods requires counseling.
Numbers: N/A

## Enteral Nutrition in Hospitalized Adults (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra2406954
Design: Narrative review
Key: Medical nutritional therapy targeting 22 kcal/kg and 0.8 g/kg protein reduces mortality in malnourished inpatients; EFFORT trial demonstrates benefit independent of caloric deficit alone.
Clinical: Proactive nutrition screening and early EN initiation reduces ICU stays and mortality; individualization by disease and baseline nutritional status required.
Numbers: EFFORT: 2000+ patients; nutritional therapy arm OR 0.65 (95% CI 0.47–0.91, p=0.01) for mortality.

## Protein Intake in Hemodialysis Patients Should Be Higher Than 1.2 g/kg/day: CON (2025)
DOI: 10.34067/KID.0000000892
Design: Debate/narrative review
Key: Intradialytic protein supplementation more effective than increasing dietary protein 1.2g/kg; amino acid losses (6-8g per session) require concurrent replacement not delayed refeeding.
Clinical: Timing of protein repletion during dialysis superior to prolonged dietary augmentation; challenges KDOQI 1.2g/kg threshold adequacy.
Numbers: Per-session dialytic amino acid loss: 6–8g (35–50g protein equivalent).

## SGLT2 Inhibitors: Multifaceted Therapeutic Agents in Cardiometabolic and Renal Diseases (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/metabo15080536
Design: Narrative review
Key: SGLT2i reduce HF hospitalization, MACE, CKD progression independent of glycemic control via natriuresis, ketone utilization, anti-inflammatory adipose remodeling, and tubuloglomerular feedback reduction.
Clinical: Beyond T2DM: proven benefit in HF (systolic/preserved), CKD, adiposopathy; emerging roles in NAFLD, PCOS; 2–4kg modest weight reduction with compensatory hyperphagia potential.
Numbers: DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced, CREDENCE, DAPA-CKD landmark trials demonstrate >20% relative risk reductions in composite endpoints.

## Athletic Pseudonephritis in Male Cross-Country Ultra-Marathoners (2025)
DOI: 10.3389/fphys.2025.1608584
Design: Observational comparative study
Key: 100km ultra-marathon induces transient hematuria, cylindruria, and proteinuria (athletic pseudonephritis); reversible without progression or long-term sequelae in trained runners.
Clinical: Proteinuria and microscopic hematuria post-exercise benign; distinguish from true glomerulonephritis; reassurance for ultramarathon athletes.
Numbers: N=20 male finishers; prevalence exercise-induced changes in 100% despite clinical absence.

## Obesity and Chronic Kidney Disease: The Dual Epidemic in Cardiovascular Health (2025)
DOI: 10.15420/ecr.2025.33
Design: Narrative review
Key: Cardio-kidney-metabolic syndrome: obesity and CKD interact via inflammation, adipokine dysregulation, RAAS/SNS activation; GLP-1 RA and SGLT2i address both phenotypes.
Clinical: Collaborative cardio-nephrology approach essential; visceral adiposity more predictive than BMI; bariatric surgery, GLP-1/SGLT2i dual therapy emerging options.
Numbers: ~50% adults overweight/obese; 1 in 10 has CKD; synergistic CV mortality risk.

## Peroxisomal Metabolism of Branched Fatty Acids Regulates Energy Homeostasis (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-09517-7
Design: Preclinical mechanistic study
Key: UCP1-independent thermogenesis via peroxisomal β-oxidation of BCAA-derived monomethyl branched-chain fatty acids (mmBCFA); ACOX2 overexpression enhances cold tolerance and metabolic homeostasis.
Clinical: Alternative thermogenic pathway to UCP1; potential target for obesity/cold intolerance; FASN and ACOX2 regulate mmBCFA cycling in brown adipose tissue.
Numbers: N/A (murine models); ACOX2 KO worsens diet-induced obesity and insulin resistance.

## Ultra-Endurance Athletes and the Metabolic Ceiling (2025)
DOI: 10.1016/j.cub.2025.08.063
Design: Observational report
Key: Maximum sustained metabolic rate plateaus near duration-dependent ceiling (~2.5× BMR at 28 weeks); 14 elite ultra-endurance athletes approach but rarely exceed proposed asymptotic limits.
Clinical: Endurance limits constrain human performance; metabolic ceiling model explains fatigue plateau in long-duration competition despite training variation.
Numbers: N=14 elite athletes; TEE 4,020–4,085 kcal/day over 52 weeks; MS 2.39±0.17 (within predicted ceiling).

## Brain Activity Associated with Breakthrough Food Preoccupation in Tirzepatide Treatment (2025)
DOI: 10.1038/s41591-025-04035-5
Design: Brief communication/case study
Key: Intracranial EEG identifies Delta-Theta power elevation in nucleus accumbens preceding food preoccupation episodes; responsive stimulation attenuates biomarker after tolerance develops.
Clinical: GLP-1 agonists may induce tolerance to hedonic suppression via mesolimbic pathway dysregulation; intracranial biomarkers identify relapse vulnerability.
Numbers: N=2 (case reports with iEEG); baseline food preoccupation Delta-Theta: p<10⁻⁶.

## Plant-Based Diets across the Spectrum of Kidney Disease (2025)
DOI: 10.2215/CJN.0000000706
Design: Narrative review
Key: Plant-based diets (Mediterranean, DASH, PLADO) reduce CKD risk via lower acid load, organic phosphate, animal protein; prospective in non-CKD and Stage 3–5 CKD.
Clinical: PBD as foundational CKD prevention and progression slowing; addresses uremic toxin burden and metabolic acidosis independent of protein dose.
Numbers: Meta-analysis 31 trials, 176,625 participants: low-sodium high-F+V high-potassium diets reduce CKD risk 22%.

## Semaglutide for Metabolic Dysfunction-Associated Steatohepatitis (Editorial) (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMe2504717
Design: Editorial
Key: ESSENCE trial semaglutide shows significant MASH resolution (62.9% vs 34.3% placebo) and fibrosis improvement; simultaneously addresses hepatic and CV-metabolic risk—unmet therapeutic need fulfilled.
Clinical: GLP-1 RA expands MASH treatment options beyond resmetirom; hepatic and systemic benefits align with MASLD's dual pathology.
Numbers: N/A (editorial on trial data).

## Phase 3 Trial of Semaglutide in Metabolic Dysfunction-Associated Steatohepatitis (ESSENCE) (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2413258
Design: RCT, Phase 3 (ESSENCE)
Key: Weekly semaglutide 2.4mg achieves MASH resolution without fibrosis worsening and fibrosis stage improvement; weight reduction (~15kg) parallels histologic benefit.
Clinical: First GLP-1 RA with robust biopsy-proven MASH efficacy; simultaneous CV-metabolic improvement; adds to limited MASH pharmacotherapy armamentarium.
Numbers: N=800 interim; MASH resolution ± no fibrosis worsening: 62.9% vs 34.3% placebo; fibrosis ≥1 grade improvement: 36.8% vs 22.4%.

## Total Energy Expenditure and Intake During a 161-km Mountain Ultramarathon (2025)
DOI: 10.1123/ijspp.2024-0535
Design: Case study
Key: Massive energy deficit during 161km ultra (5000–6000 kcal expended, ~2500 ingested); compensatory hyperphagia and energy debt recovery occur post-event.
Clinical: Ultramarathon fueling strategies inadequate for real-time balance; post-event energy surplus prediction and recovery nutrition timing optimize adaptation.
Numbers: N=2 runners; mean TEE 5,900±400 kcal over 24–33 hours; intake ~50% expenditure.

## Revising BMI Cut-Off Points for Overweight and Obesity in Male Athletes (2025)
DOI: 10.3390/nu17050908
Design: Cross-sectional observational
Key: WHO BMI thresholds (≥25 overweight, ≥30 obese) misclassify muscular athletes; proposed higher cutoffs (≥28.2, ≥33.7 kg/m²) align better with BF%-based classification.
Clinical: Sport-specific BMI interpretation necessary to avoid false obesity diagnosis in high-muscle-mass populations; body composition assessment supplements BMI.
Numbers: N=622 male athletes; WHO overestimated overweight/obesity prevalence; new thresholds demonstrate improved specificity with BF%.

## Adipocyte Regulation of Insulin Sensitivity and the Risk of Type 2 Diabetes (2023)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMra2216691
Design: Narrative review
Key: Adipocyte GLUT4 downregulation in obesity → systemic insulin resistance via RBP4, ChREBP, and PAHSAs; FAHFAs (especially PAHSAs) transduce metabolic dysfunction across liver-muscle-β-cell axis.
Clinical: Adipocyte insulin sensitivity as therapeutic target; FAHFA pathways and receptor agonism (GPR120, GPR40) potential precision approaches to T2D.
Numbers: N/A

## Steatotic Liver Disease (2024)
DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(24)01811-7
Design: Seminar/narrative review
Key: MASLD affects 33% globally; fibrosis progression predicts mortality; biomarkers enable early detection; resmetirom approved, GLP-1/SGLT2i under investigation.
Clinical: Unified diagnostic framework (MASLD vs ALD vs MetALD) streamlines primary-specialty triage; non-invasive fibrosis assessment prioritizes intervention.
Numbers: 33% global prevalence MASLD; cirrhosis 11th leading cause death; 2–4% mortality attributable.

## Lipoprotein(a) and Effects of Diet: Time for Reassessment (2025)

Design: Narrative review
Key: Dietary saturated fat reduction paradoxically raises Lp(a) despite LDL-C lowering; Lp(a) size polymorphism moderates response; future management must consider Lp(a)-specific dietary effects.
Clinical: Lp(a) management distinct from LDL; dietary counseling inadequate as sole intervention; emerging drugs achieve 98% Lp(a) reduction warranting clinical trials.
Numbers: N/A
