# Public Health Policy — Journal Reading Wiki

## Aspirin for Primary CVD Prevention in CKD (2021)
DOI: 10.1093/eurjpc/zwab132
Design: Systematic review & meta-analysis
Key: Aspirin confers no CVD/mortality benefit in CKD patients eGFR 15–60; increases major bleeding. Risk exceeds benefit.
Clinical: Not recommended for primary CVD prevention in CKD; alternatives (SGLT2i, lipid control, BP reduction) preferred.
Numbers: Increased major bleeding; CVD events not reduced.

## 24-Hour Urinary Sodium/Potassium & Cardiovascular Risk (2022)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2109794
Design: Prospective cohort & individual-level meta-analysis
Key: Multiple 24h urine samples more accurate than single samples; low sodium–CVD association likely confounded by reverse causation/underlying illness.
Clinical: Assess sodium/potassium intake via repeated 24h collections, not spot samples; interpret low values cautiously.
Numbers: Methodological study; 6 cohorts, ~2M participants.

## SGLT2 Inhibitors & Genital/UTI Infection Risk (2025)
DOI: 10.4103/tcmj.tcmj_275_24
Design: Narrative review
Key: SGLT2i increase genital infections significantly; UTI risk modest. Most infections mild; severe cases rare. CV/renal benefits outweigh risk.
Clinical: Use SGLT2i in indicated patients; emphasize hygiene, monitor for infection signs, manage symptomatically.
Numbers: Genital infections elevated; UTI slightly elevated; serious infections <5%.

Clinical: Nephrologists lead prevention & risk assessment; early screening & intervention critical for population health.

## Global Lifetime Cardiovascular Risk Factor Impact (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2415879
Design: Cohort & meta-analysis, 133 cohorts, 2.1M individuals
Key: Absence of 5 major risk factors (HTN, dyslipidemia, smoking, DM, BMI) at age 50 extends CVD-free lifespan 10+ years. Early HTN/smoking control most beneficial.
Clinical: Mid-age risk factor correction essential; smoking cessation & BP control yield maximum health gain.
Numbers: 13.3y CVD-free bonus (women), 10.6y (men); 38% lifetime CVD risk with all 5 factors.

## Multiplicity Control in Clinical Trials (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/EVIDctw2400393
Design: Educational review
Key: Multiple hypothesis testing inflates Type I error (FWER); Bonferroni, Holm stepping, graphical testing preserve alpha.
Clinical: Mandatory for trials with ≥2 primary endpoints; transparent multiplicity strategy required.
Numbers: Traditional alpha = 0.05; divided across hypotheses.

## Total & Specific Potato Intake & Type 2 Diabetes Risk (2025)
DOI: 10.1136/bmj-2024-082121
Design: 3-cohort prospective study & meta-analysis
Key: Total potatoes & french fries increase T2D risk; boiled/baked not associated. Whole grain substitution protective.
Clinical: Counsel reducing processed potatoes, increase whole grains. Preparation method matters.
Numbers: +5% per 3 weekly servings (potatoes); +20% per 3 servings (fries); −19% with grain substitution.

## Sample Size Determination for Pilot Trials (2025)
DOI: 10.1136/bmj-2024-083405
Design: Tutorial/methodology review
Key: Pilot trials assess feasibility, not efficacy; sample size targets progression criteria, not power for primary hypothesis.
Clinical: Use pilot results to plan definitive trial; 3 designs per CONSORT pilot extension.
Numbers: Tutorials provided; typical pilots 30–100 per arm depending on outcome.

## Knee Osteoarthritis Exercise Modalities—Comparative Efficacy (2025)
DOI: 10.1136/bmj-2025-085242
Design: Systematic review & network meta-analysis, 217 RCTs
Key: Aerobic exercise most effective for pain, function, gait, QOL in knee OA (moderate evidence). Mind-body & neuromotor exercise also beneficial.
Clinical: Recommend aerobic exercise first-line; tailor modality to patient tolerance & preference.
Numbers: 217 RCTs, 15,684 participants; aerobic superior at 12–24 weeks.

Design: Perspective
Key: IHS patients lack GINA genetic discrimination protections (unlike VA, military, federal employees). Limits genomic medicine access & research participation.
Clinical: Advocate for GINA extension to IHS; blocks equitable personalized medicine delivery.
Numbers: Affects millions of Native Americans; genetic tests available but access limited by mistrust.

## Sotatercept in High-Risk PAH (ZENITH Trial) (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2415160
Design: RCT (Phase 3, double-blind)
Key: Sotatercept 76% risk reduction for death/transplant/PAH hospitalization vs placebo in advanced PAH despite max background therapy.
Clinical: First activin inhibitor for PAH; approved for WHO III–IV patients with high REVEAL score.
Numbers: 172 patients; 17.4% sotatercept vs 54.7% placebo reached primary endpoint; RR 0.24 (p<0.001).

## Endometriosis: A Review (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.2975
Design: Clinical review
Key: 10% of reproductive-age women; 90% with pelvic pain, 26% infertile. Diagnosis delayed 5–12y. Hormonal therapy first-line; surgery for refractory.
Clinical: Screen for endometriosis in dysmenorrhea/pelvic pain; early treatment improves QOL & fertility outcomes.
Numbers: 10% prevalence; ~9M US women affected; $78B annual US cost (treatment+lost productivity).

## Navigational Bronchoscopy vs Transthoracic Biopsy for Lung Nodules (VERITAS) (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2414059
Design: RCT (Phase 3, non-inferiority)
Key: Navigational bronchoscopy non-inferior to transthoracic biopsy for nodules 10–30mm; pneumothorax risk 2% vs 25%, superior safety.
Clinical: Prefer navigational bronchoscopy when available; lower morbidity, equivalent diagnostic yield.
Numbers: Diagnostic accuracy ~90% both arms; pneumothorax 2% vs 25%.

## Non-Cystic Fibrosis Bronchiectasis in Adults (2025)
DOI: 10.1001/jama.2025.2680
Design: Clinical review
Key: ~500K US adults; airway clearance + antibiotics core therapy; long-term macrolide/inhaled antibiotic for frequent exacerbations.
Clinical: Early diagnosis improves outcomes; teach symptom recognition (purulent sputum) for timely antibiotic initiation.
Numbers: ~500K US prevalence; diagnostic delay 5–10y in 15%; 812/100K in age ≥75.

## Twice-Yearly Lenacapavir for HIV Prevention (PURPOSE 2) (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2411858
Design: RCT (Phase 3, double-blind, active-control)
Key: Lenacapavir 89% more effective than daily F/TDF; 96% reduction vs background in high-risk MSM & gender-diverse persons.
Clinical: Highest-efficacy PrEP option; superior adherence profile; 2 breakthrough infections with resistance mutations.
Numbers: 0.10/100py lenacapavir vs 0.93/100py F/TDF vs 2.37/100py background incidence.

## Vitamin D, Omega-3 & Home Exercise for Sarcopenia (DO-HEALTH) (2024–2025)
DOI: 10.1111/jgs.19266
Design: RCT (3-year, 2×2×2 factorial)
Key: In healthy, active older adults, vitamin D, omega-3, and home exercise alone or combined did not reduce sarcopenia or improve ALMI.
Clinical: Interventions ineffective in this population; may require higher exercise intensity or protein supplementation.
Numbers: 3-year follow-up; no difference in ALMI or sarcopenia prevalence.

## US Research Leadership at Crossroads—NIH Indirect Cost Cuts (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMp2502451
Design: Perspective
Key: Proposed 15% ICR cap threatens research infrastructure. Actual institutional ICRs 38–60%; cap = multimillion-dollar loss per institution.
Clinical: Indirect costs fund facilities, compliance, research support essential for translational pipeline. Cuts jeopardize future breakthroughs.
Numbers: 99.4% FDA-approved therapies (2010–2019) trace to NIH-supported basic research.

## Addressing Alcohol Use (2025)
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMcp2402121
Design: Clinical review
Key: Unhealthy alcohol use major US health burden (178K deaths/yr). Screening + brief intervention effective; pharmacotherapy (naltrexone, acamprosate, topiramate) available.
Clinical: Screen all patients; intervene early for risky drinking; refer to addiction services for AUD.
Numbers: 10.6% US adults with AUD; 23.5% recent binge drinking.
